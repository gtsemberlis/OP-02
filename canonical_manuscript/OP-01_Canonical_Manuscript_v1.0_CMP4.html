Operation Overview

Return to contents

A modern aviation-safety system built by Alexandria Aviation Systems begins producing verified runway-obstruction warnings that no human observer can explain. The detections survive comparison across radar, lidar, optical, infrared, and operational data. One warning contributes to a violent hard landing at Chicago O’Hare, leaving injuries and fatalities.

The investigators must determine whether the detections are genuine, what is being observed, whether it presents a threat, how knowledge of it should be contained, and whether Alexandria can continue operating its technology.

The truth is not a malicious machine or a conventional conspiracy. Alexandria has inadvertently developed a way to observe a naturally occurring ecology that has always existed outside ordinary human perception. The organisms themselves appear harmless. The danger comes from perception, institutional reaction, and the consequences of acting on evidence that human beings were never equipped to receive.

Operational Objectives

  • Verify the anomaly.
  • Determine its nature.
  • Assess whether it poses a threat.
  • Establish a containment or mitigation protocol.
  • Evaluate whether Alexandria may continue its work.

Handler Principles

  • Reward observation over interrogation.
  • Build confidence through independent corroboration rather than a single decisive clue.
  • Portray competent institutions as competent.
  • Let mechanics deepen understanding rather than gate necessary information.
  • Keep violence brief and consequential.
  • Preserve uncertainty even after confirmation.
  • End the operation with judgment, not conquest.

Scene 1 — Cold Open: O’Hare Hard Landing

Return to contents

A commercial flight makes a violent hard landing at O’Hare after its crew responds to a verified runway-obstruction warning that no human observer can explain.

Purpose: Establish the human cost, institutional stakes, and central contradiction: independent systems agreed that something occupied the runway.

Location: Chicago O’Hare International Airport
Participants: Karen Delgado, Mark Reynolds, Emily Chen, airport and airline personnel
Estimated Runtime: 20–35 minutes
Investigation Phase: Verification

Scene Flow

  1. Present the immediate aftermath without revealing the phenomenon.
  2. Allow investigators to review the aircraft, runway response, preliminary reports, and surviving crew accounts.
  3. Establish that the warning was neither a single-sensor error nor ordinary pilot confusion.
  4. Transition to the Program briefing and the Alexandria connection.

Key Information

  • The aircraft received a credible runway-obstruction warning during final approach.
  • The obstruction was corroborated across systems expected to fail independently.
  • The evasive response caused the hard landing, injuries, and fatalities.
  • No physical debris, vehicle, animal, or conventional obstruction was recovered.

Evidence: O’Hare Incident Summary.

Handler Guidance: Keep the scene procedural and humane. The incident is frightening because competent people reacted reasonably to evidence that should not exist.

Before Leaving: Investigators should understand that the event is real, consequential, and linked to Alexandria’s sensor-fusion platform.

Scene 2 — Program Briefing

Return to contents

The Program assigns the investigators to determine whether the obstruction reports are genuine, dangerous, containable, and compatible with Alexandria’s continued operation.

Purpose: Define authority, scope, constraints, and the Program’s reactive posture.

Location: Program Secure Briefing Facility
Participants: Michael Havel and the investigators
Estimated Runtime: 15–25 minutes
Investigation Phase: Assignment

Scene Flow

  1. Review the O’Hare event and related anomaly reports.
  2. State the operational objectives and cover identities.
  3. Clarify that the Program has no settled explanation.
  4. Provide access to Alexandria and relevant federal partners.

Key Information

  • O’Hare is one of several geographically separated incidents.
  • Three reports involve military airfields; others occurred over the Atlantic and in Alaska.
  • The common factor is independent multi-modal confirmation.
  • The Program wants assessment and containment, not automatic destruction.

Evidence: Initial Program Briefing.

Handler Guidance: Do not portray the Program as omniscient. Havel knows the pattern qualifies for attention, not what it means. He is calm, patient, and evidence-first.

Before Leaving: Investigators should have a clear mandate and a reason to begin with Alexandria.

Scene 3 — Institutional Coordination

Return to contents

Investigators enter Alexandria under official aviation-safety cover, meet its leadership, and establish access to the system and its personnel.

Purpose: Present Alexandria as competent and non-malicious while exposing the breadth of the anomaly.

Location: Alexandria Aviation Systems Headquarters
Participants: Ethan Mercer, Kevin Park, Javier Ortiz
Estimated Runtime: 35–60 minutes
Investigation Phase: Access and Orientation

Scene Flow

  1. Mercer frames Alexandria’s mission and reputational risk.
  2. Park explains the sensor-fusion architecture and validation safeguards.
  3. Ortiz establishes access limits and internal-security concerns.
  4. Investigators review the technical overview and identify Dr. Ananya Rao as the person closest to the anomaly.

Key Information

  • Alexandria’s system fuses radar, lidar, optical, infrared, and operational data.
  • The alerts survived internal validation and were not generated by one defective modality.
  • The company has attempted to reproduce, suppress, and explain the results.
  • Bird activity around active equipment has changed in subtle but repeatable ways.

Evidence: Alexandria Technical Overview.

Handler Guidance: Alexandria should reward professional treatment. Suspicion is reasonable, but evidence should steadily undermine the idea of deliberate fraud.

Before Leaving: Investigators should understand the system well enough to recognize why ordinary malfunction is inadequate.

Scene 4 — Alexandria Systems Review

Return to contents

Investigators examine raw incidents, internal tests, and physical operating conditions to determine whether Alexandria’s anomaly is reproducible.

Purpose: Convert suspicion into verified evidence and introduce the phenomenon’s wider ecology.

Location: Alexandria Aviation Systems Headquarters
Participants: Dr. Ananya Rao, Kevin Park, Javier Ortiz
Estimated Runtime: 45–75 minutes
Investigation Phase: Technical Verification

Scene Flow

  1. Compare unrelated obstruction events.
  2. Review modality timestamps and confidence thresholds.
  3. Inspect equipment, access logs, and data provenance.
  4. Observe the bird-avoidance pattern and discuss controlled testing.

Key Information

  • The incidents are not copies of one another and do not share a simple data-source failure.
  • Confidence increases when more modalities are added rather than collapsing.
  • The anomaly appears in multiple forms and scales.
  • Active Alexandria systems correlate with subtle animal avoidance.

Evidence: Runway Obstruction Event Summary.

Handler Guidance: Allow investigators to disprove mundane explanations. Failure should cost time or create uncertainty, not hide the core truth.

Before Leaving: The investigators should accept that the system is observing a real external phenomenon.

Scene 5 — Dr. Rao and Independent Analysis

Return to contents

Rao shares the private research that has carried her beyond Alexandria’s official conclusions and reveals the personal cost of sustained exposure.

Purpose: Humanize the discovery, establish the scientific model, and set up Rao’s later unauthorized action.

Location: Dr. Rao’s Office
Participants: Dr. Ananya Rao
Estimated Runtime: 35–60 minutes
Investigation Phase: Interpretation

Scene Flow

  1. Rao explains how she separated the anomaly from model behavior.
  2. She shows notebooks containing sketches, comparisons, and speculative biological language.
  3. Investigators notice obsession, sleep loss, and perceptual fixation without incoherence.
  4. Their treatment of Rao determines whether she later trusts them with her notes.

Key Information

  • Rao believes the system is detecting living organisms outside normal human perception.
  • Large runway forms and smaller plankton-like forms may be part of the same ecology.
  • The technology may make biological observers fractionally more capable of sensing the organisms.
  • Rao has begun to perceive traces without looking directly at processed output.

Evidence: Dr. Rao’s Research Notebook.

Handler Guidance: Rao is rational, frightened, and obsessed—not a prophetic mad scientist. Her conclusions should be testable even when incomplete.

Before Leaving: Investigators should possess or know how to obtain Rao’s research and understand why she may break protocol.

Scene 6 — Independent Witnesses

Return to contents

Investigators interview survivors and observers whose incomplete accounts independently support the technical evidence.

Purpose: Reward attention, broaden the human perspective, and provide non-technical corroboration without a single decisive witness.

Location: NTSB Field Office
Participants: Karen Delgado, Emily Chen, Lisa Morales, Robert Gallagher, Sarah Walker, Owen Walker
Estimated Runtime: 45–75 minutes
Investigation Phase: Independent Corroboration

Ways of Observing

  • The frequent flyer contributes routine and pattern recognition.
  • The aviation enthusiast contributes technical awareness and communications context.
  • The flight attendant contributes professional behavior and crew response.
  • The injured passenger contributes human cost and emotional reality.
  • The child contributes unfiltered perception.

Children draw what they see. Adults decide what it means.

Key Information

  • The crew reacted to instrument agreement, not a fleeting visual impression.
  • Several witnesses noticed motion or turbulence inconsistent with an empty runway.
  • Details align across people who did not coordinate their accounts.
  • Owen’s drawing resembles the large form inferred from Rao’s data.

Evidence: Owen Walker’s Drawing and witness statements.

Handler Guidance: Do not gate the drawing behind a perfect question. Reward investigators who notice Owen, his papers, or Sarah’s concern. No interview gives the answer; meaning emerges from comparing overlapping observations.

Before Leaving: Investigators should have human corroboration independent of Alexandria.

Scene 7 — The Collectors

Return to contents

Rao contacts the investigators while hiding from unknown intruders seeking her notebooks. The confrontation reveals that another organization has prior knowledge of the phenomenon.

Purpose: Introduce external pressure, force immediate action, and establish historical awareness without resolving the Collectors’ identity.

Location: Dr. Rao’s office or residence; Alexandria if preferred
Participants: Dr. Ananya Rao, Javier Ortiz, the Collectors
Estimated Runtime: 30–60 minutes
Investigation Phase: Escalation

Scene Flow

  1. Rao makes a frightened but coherent call.
  2. Investigators arrive as two or three Collectors withdraw.
  3. A brief confrontation or pursuit occurs; one Collector may die.
  4. The body and recovered fragments reveal repaired gear, no electronics, and field notes about similar observations.

Key Information

  • The Collectors expected handwritten material and avoided electronic equipment.
  • Their clothing and boots are maintained rather than disposable.
  • Their notebook contains diagrams and symbols consistent with long-term field observation.
  • Nothing recovered identifies a clear sponsor.

Evidence: Collector Notebook Fragments.

Handler Guidance: Keep combat short and consequential. The scene’s purpose is evidence and pressure, not defeating the organization.

Before Leaving: Rao should be safe or in custody, the notebook fragments secured, and the investigators aware they are not the first observers.

Scene 8 — Runway Observation

Return to contents

Investigators authorize or conduct a controlled Alexandria test that produces direct multi-modal confirmation of the large runway organism and its surrounding ecology.

Purpose: Deliver the operation’s experiential climax and answer whether the phenomenon is real and immediately hostile.

Location: Chicago O’Hare International Airport or another controlled airfield
Participants: Dr. Ananya Rao, Kevin Park, Karen Delgado, investigators
Estimated Runtime: 45–75 minutes
Investigation Phase: Controlled Verification

Scene Flow

  1. Define safety controls and abort conditions.
  2. Activate the Alexandria system and compare live modalities.
  3. The large manta-like form resolves across instrumentation while smaller forms move around it.
  4. Human observers may experience partial biological perception, followed by a dangerous but avoidable operational moment.

Key Information

  • The phenomenon is physical enough to interact with multiple sensor modalities.
  • The large organism does not attack and appears indifferent to aircraft and humans.
  • Smaller organisms cluster or drift around active observation zones.
  • Observation may increase biological awareness without granting reliable normal sight.

Evidence: Alexandria Runway Observation Record.

Handler Guidance: Reveal enough to confirm, not enough to domesticate the mystery. The organisms are harmless; human systems create the danger.

Before Leaving: Investigators should be able to assess threat, containment, and Alexandria’s future.

Variant: The test may be refused. Investigators can reach a defensible conclusion through accumulated evidence and historical comparison.

Scene 9 — Comparative Assessment and Debrief

Return to contents

The investigators compare the operation to incomplete Program precedents, decide Alexandria’s disposition, and define what the Program inherits from the discovery.

Purpose: Turn revelation into an ethical and institutional decision rather than a monster-ending.

Location: Program Secure Briefing Facility
Participants: Michael Havel, investigators, optional Dr. Ananya Rao
Estimated Runtime: 30–60 minutes
Investigation Phase: Assessment and Containment

Scene Flow

  1. Review the verified evidence and unresolved risks.
  2. Havel provides a comparative file showing earlier independent-confirmation cases.
  3. Investigators recommend shutdown, controlled continuation, concealment, protection, or a mixed protocol.
  4. Record consequences for Rao, Alexandria, the Collectors, and future operations.

Key Information

  • The Program has encountered adjacent patterns but not a complete explanation.
  • No evidence proves the organisms are hostile.
  • Alexandria’s technology can reveal them and may alter human or animal perception.
  • Containment is primarily informational, procedural, and institutional.

Evidence: Comparative Case File.

Handler Guidance: Treat the decision as genuinely open. Each option protects something and endangers something else. The operation ends when the investigators make and communicate a defensible recommendation.

Possible Outcomes

  • Alexandria is shut down and its work suppressed.
  • Alexandria continues under strict controls.
  • The Program quietly assumes stewardship of the technology.
  • Rao is protected, detained, hospitalized, recruited, or separated from the research.
  • The operation closes with an unresolved institutional compromise.

Appendix F — Mechanical Reference

Return to contents

Core Principle

Mechanics determine the quality of understanding, not access to the operation.

Information Tiers

Tier 1 — Always Available: Core facts necessary to continue.
Tier 2 — Expertise: Detail that eliminates alternatives or improves confidence.
Tier 3 — Insight: Connections and implications earned through strong play or successful skill use.

Situation Suggested Skills
Aviation evidence review Search, Bureaucracy
Alexandria technical discussion Science (Engineering), SIGINT, Computer Science
Interviewing witnesses HUMINT, Persuade
Reading Rao’s notebooks Science, Search
Collector encounter Alertness, Firearms, Athletics
Runway observation Alertness, Science
Final assessment Bureaucracy, Persuade

Failure may delay a conclusion, add cost, create uncertainty, damage trust, or expose investigators to risk. It should not halt the investigation or conceal mandatory information.

The horror of Airspace is epistemological. SAN loss should accompany genuine confrontation with the limits of reliable perception, not every technical anomaly.

Seven Principles of Running Airspace

  1. Observation over interrogation.
  2. Confidence over clue chains.
  3. Competent institutions.
  4. Mechanics deepen understanding.
  5. Restrained presentation.
  6. Let players conclude first.
  7. End with judgment.
