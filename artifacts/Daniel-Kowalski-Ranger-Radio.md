# A-08 — Daniel Kowalski Ranger Radio

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Artifact: Daniel Kowalski's Ranger Radio
## Description

A rugged, handheld VHF/UHF public safety radio issued to Daniel Kowalski as part of his duties with the New York State Park system.

The radio is black, waterproof, and visibly worn from years of outdoor use.

Minor cosmetic damage includes:

Light scratches across the housing.
Scuffed rubber antenna.
Worn push-to-talk button.
Dirt embedded around the battery compartment.

The radio otherwise appears well maintained.

Its battery remains partially charged.

Investigators typically discover it abandoned somewhere within the wooded area surrounding the Hogsback Ranger Station.

It appears to have been dropped rather than deliberately discarded.

## Who Possesses It?

Initially, no one.

The radio lies where it fell until investigators recover it.

Once recovered, it becomes one of the few portable pieces of physical evidence that investigators can repeatedly examine throughout the operation.

## Where Is It Found?

The radio is located somewhere within the wooded area surrounding the Hogsback Ranger Station.

Its exact position depends upon how the investigation unfolds, but it is generally discovered along the route Daniel Kowalski likely traveled shortly before disappearing.

No obvious signs of violence accompany its discovery.

## Initial Interpretation

At first glance, the radio appears completely ordinary.

Its condition is consistent with routine field use.

Investigators naturally assume it was dropped during Kowalski's disappearance.

Nothing immediately suggests sabotage, damage, or foul play.

Only after powering the unit on does its significance become apparent.

## Further Examination
Electronics

The radio is fully functional.

No internal components are damaged.

Battery voltage remains normal.

Transmitter and receiver both operate within expected specifications.

Nothing suggests equipment failure.

Communications / Radio Operation

The programmed channels correspond to normal park operations.

No unauthorized frequencies have been added.

No evidence exists that the radio has been modified.

When monitoring certain channels, investigators begin hearing persistent bursts of unusual interference.

Unlike ordinary static, the interference possesses structure.

It is neither voice nor conventional digital transmission.

Experienced radio operators immediately recognize that the signal is real.

They simply cannot identify its source.

Signal Analysis

The recovered radio provides perhaps the clearest reception of the anomalous signal encountered during the investigation.

Compared with Hal Whitaker's recording, the interference is:

stronger,
more consistent,
richer in harmonic detail,
less affected by background noise.

Repeated listening confirms that the signal is not random.

It contains recurring structures suggestive of deliberate transmission.

Whether those structures represent communication, environmental effects, or something else entirely remains unknown.

Physics / Engineering

Nothing about the radio explains the phenomenon.

The anomalous signal exists independently of the equipment receiving it.

Different radios tuned appropriately reproduce essentially the same interference.

Daniel's radio merely happened to be closer to its source.

## Relationships

Daniel Kowalski's radio reinforces several other artifacts without duplicating them.

Compared alongside:

Hal Whitaker's radio recording,
Nathaniel Collins' signal analysis,
Samantha Carter's field observations,
Brooks' comparative investigations,
SaucerWatch reports of unusual radio interference,

a consistent pattern begins to emerge.

The radio does not identify the source.

It demonstrates that multiple independent observers have encountered the same measurable phenomenon.

## Investigation Impact

Daniel Kowalski's radio transforms the anomalous signal from recorded evidence into an active investigative tool.

Unlike Hal Whitaker's recording, which captures the signal at a single moment in time, the recovered radio allows investigators to monitor the phenomenon directly while moving through the forest.

The signal varies subtly with location.

At times it strengthens.

At times it fades.

Occasionally its character changes in ways no investigator can readily explain.

Although insufficient to function as a conventional tracking device, careful comparison of signal strength, environmental conditions, and terrain can help investigators narrow their search toward areas warranting closer examination.

The radio never points directly toward the Mi-Go chamber.

Instead, it rewards systematic observation and disciplined fieldwork.

Used carelessly, it produces only strange static.

Used intelligently, it becomes another navigational clue.

## Changes Over Time

The radio itself does not change.

The signal does.

As events unfold within Hogsback State Forest, investigators may notice subtle variations in:

signal strength,
harmonic structure,
duration,
periods of complete silence.

Whether these changes reflect movement, environmental conditions, or activity within the forest remains impossible to determine.

If investigators revisit previously explored areas, they may discover that the signal no longer behaves exactly as they remember.

Like the phenomenon itself, it resists becoming entirely predictable.

## Handler Notes

Daniel Kowalski's radio exists to reinforce one of the operation's central themes:

The Unnatural is measurable before it is understandable.

The investigators possess an instrument capable of detecting a genuine physical anomaly.

That does not mean they understand what they are measuring.

The radio should never become a mythos "radar."

Instead, it rewards careful comparison with other evidence gathered throughout the investigation.

Its greatest value lies not in revealing answers, but in encouraging investigators to correlate independent observations into an increasingly coherent picture of the forest.

Like much of the operation's evidence, the radio demonstrates that observation is objective, while interpretation remains uncertain.
