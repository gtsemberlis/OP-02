# Samantha Carter's Field Journal

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Artifact: Samantha Carter's Field Journal
Description

A well-used 7" × 10" hardcover notebook bound in faded pea-green textile.

Its cream-colored pages are pre-numbered with a faint dot grid, although Samantha almost never writes neatly enough to follow it. Most pages contain diagrams, arrows, sketches, marginal notes, and references that spill freely across the paper. Despite its appearance, the notebook is remarkably organized. Individual entries frequently reference earlier observations by page number, creating an interconnected web of cross-references that allows Samantha to revisit previous ideas without rewriting them.

The journal is not a diary in the traditional sense.

Instead, it functions as a field notebook.

Its contents include:

Amateur radio observations.
Nature photography logs.
Hiking notes.
Club meeting summaries.
College radio station projects.
Engineering sketches.
Planned hiking routes.
Wildlife observations.
Small environmental anomalies.

Many locations are identified only by shorthand or colloquial names rather than official trail names or map coordinates.

Scattered among these technical observations are brief personal reflections—not emotional confessions, but observations about uncertainty, curiosity, and occasional frustration when she cannot explain something she has noticed.

Nate Collins is mentioned regularly as a mentor and sounding board.

Their relationship is consistently portrayed as academic, collaborative, and entirely appropriate.

Who Possesses It?

Samantha Carter.

The journal is one of her most valued possessions.

She rarely goes anywhere without it.

Because she views it as a personal working notebook rather than evidence, she is reluctant to surrender it to investigators.

Persuading her to share it requires establishing trust or demonstrating that the investigation genuinely benefits from her observations.

Alternatively, investigators might obtain the journal without permission, though doing so risks damaging their relationship with Samantha.

Where Is It Found?

Most commonly:

Samantha's bedroom at the Carter residence.
Her backpack while attending classes at Mohawk Valley Community College.
On her person while hiking.

If investigators resort to covert methods, the notebook is small enough to steal or discreetly photograph, though Samantha is observant enough that she may eventually notice it has been disturbed.

Initial Interpretation

The journal immediately humanizes Samantha.

Rather than revealing hidden secrets, it portrays an intelligent, disciplined young woman with an unusually observant mind.

The entries surrounding the disappearances naturally draw investigators' attention.

Hal Whittaker's disappearance is recorded with genuine concern, particularly because Samantha worries about his age and recent widowhood.

Christopher Donnelly's and David Barlow's disappearances produce increasingly urgent entries, but Samantha never leaps to extraordinary conclusions.

She records facts.

She asks questions.

She continues living her life.

Throughout these entries are occasional notes from hikes conducted with portable radio equipment.

Most describe routine observations.

One entry, however, simply reads:

Overlook?

Beneath it are several pages of confused technical observations concerning unusual radio behavior and inconsistent signal measurements.

Nothing identifies the location explicitly.

At first glance, it appears to be an abandoned line of inquiry.

Further Examination

The journal rewards careful interdisciplinary analysis rather than any single expertise.

Military Communications / SIGINT

Investigators recognize that Samantha unknowingly recorded observations consistent with the same unusual frequency identified in Hal Whitaker's recording.

Her terminology differs from Hal's, but the phenomenon appears identical.

Cartography / Navigation / Survival

Comparing Samantha's sketches with local trail maps allows investigators to identify several parks she regularly visited.

Importantly, Hogsback is only one of several possibilities.

The journal narrows possibilities without identifying a destination.

Nature / Forestry

Photographs and environmental notes reveal subtle but useful information.

Tree species, glacial rock formations, drainage patterns, seasonal vegetation, and sunlight angles allow experienced outdoorsmen to estimate terrain type, approximate elevation, and even likely times of day for certain observations.

Photography

Analysis of Samantha's photographs reveals details she never commented upon directly.

Repeated landmarks.

Consistent camera orientations.

Patterns in where she chose to stop and observe.

The photographs become navigational evidence as much as artistic records.

Engineering / Mathematics

Samantha's measurements display remarkable consistency.

Although she never reaches definitive conclusions, investigators may recognize that she was unconsciously documenting repeatable physical phenomena rather than subjective impressions.

Psychology

The notebook contains no evidence of paranoia, obsession, delusion, or deteriorating mental health.

If anything, Samantha becomes more methodical as events grow stranger.

This greatly increases the credibility of her observations.

Relationships

The journal quietly connects much of the investigation.

It references:

Hal Whitaker.
Nate Collins.
Christopher Donnelly.
David Barlow.
Amateur Radio Club meetings.
Mohawk Valley Community College.
Hogsback State Forest.
Several neighboring parks.

Compared alongside Hal Whitaker's radio recording, Samantha's hiking notes help investigators begin excluding possible transmitter locations.

Compared with Nate Collins' evidence package, her observations often provide environmental context for Nate's technical data.

Compared with Daniel Kowalski's recordings, investigators begin recognizing recurring locations where unusual radio behavior appears repeatedly.

The journal does not reveal the Mi-Go chamber.

It gradually teaches investigators where not to search.

Investigation Impact

The journal advances the investigation in two distinct ways.

First, it becomes one of the operation's most valuable comparative artifacts.

Its observations, when combined with technical evidence from Hal Whitaker, Nate Collins, and Daniel Kowalski, allow investigators to progressively constrain the geographic area from which the anomalous signal originates.

The journal rewards synthesis rather than isolated analysis.

Second, the journal possesses an unusual characteristic shared by no other artifact in the operation.

It is alive.

If Samantha retains possession after meeting the investigators, she continues writing.

Subsequent entries begin documenting:

New observations.
Revised theories.
Additional hiking plans.
Reactions to investigators' questions.
Questions she now believes deserve answers.

If SaucerWatch arrives in the area, Samantha may briefly note their presence and record cautious impressions of their methods.

She neither embraces nor dismisses them outright.

She simply observes.

As the investigation progresses, the notebook quietly evolves into a parallel investigation conducted entirely by Samantha herself.

Changes Over Time

Unlike most artifacts, Samantha's Field Journal is dynamic.

Every day Samantha remains free to investigate, new pages may be added.

These additions should never solve the investigation.

Instead, they reflect Samantha's own growing understanding of the mystery.

If investigators repeatedly consult the journal over multiple days, they witness another intelligent observer independently approaching many of the same conclusions they have reached.

If Samantha disappears before the notebook is recovered, the journal instead becomes a snapshot of an investigation permanently interrupted.

Its unfinished pages become far more poignant than any completed explanation could have been.

Handler Notes

Samantha's Field Journal is designed to reward one of the operation's central themes:

Observation before explanation.

Unlike Brooks, Samantha rarely theorizes.

Unlike Nate, she rarely models systems.

Unlike Hal, she rarely performs formal analysis.

She simply records the world with exceptional care.

Many of her observations seem trivial when encountered individually.

Only after investigators compare them with other artifacts do their true significance emerge.

The notebook should never function as a hidden answer key.

Instead, it should demonstrate that patient, disciplined observation can carry an investigator remarkably close to the truth—even when they do not yet understand what they are seeing.

Who Possesses It?

Samantha Carter.

Establishing trust—or demonstrating genuine interest in her observations—is usually required before she willingly shares it.

Investigators could obtain it without her knowledge, but doing so risks permanently damaging their relationship with her if discovered.

Where Is It Found?

The notebook is most commonly found:

On Samantha's desk or bookshelf in her bedroom at the Carter residence.
Inside her backpack while attending classes at Mohawk Valley Community College.
In her hiking pack during field excursions.

Because Samantha carries it so consistently, investigators often have only brief opportunities to examine it unless she willingly cooperates.

Investigation Impact

The journal advances the investigation in two distinct ways.

Second, the notebook's evidentiary value changes over time because Samantha continues writing in it.

Each day she remains free, new observations may appear:

Questions prompted by conversations with investigators.
Additional radio measurements.
New hiking plans.
Revised interpretations of earlier observations.
Brief comments regarding SaucerWatch's arrival and methods.

On July 2, the tone changes for the first time.

Samantha writes that she has decided she needs to go back to "where it happened."

She does not explain precisely where she means.

The accompanying pages contain a carefully organized equipment checklist unlike anything elsewhere in the notebook.

Rather than her usual lightweight hiking gear, the list includes supplies appropriate for an extended wilderness survival excursion:

Topographic maps.
Compass.
Extra food and water.
Emergency shelter.
First aid supplies.
Spare batteries.
Flashlights.
Additional radio equipment.
Fire-starting materials.
Rope.
Utility knife.
Extra clothing.

The list is practical, thoughtful, and unsettling.

It reads less like preparations for a hike and more like preparations for someone who expects they may not return the same day.

To Samantha, it is simply prudent planning.

To investigators who have come to understand the pattern of disappearances, it may become one of the clearest warnings that she intends to place herself directly back in harm's way.

Changes Over Time

The journal itself never changes.

Its significance does.

Recovered early, it is simply the working notebook of a gifted student.

Recovered later, it becomes an increasingly current record of Samantha's own parallel investigation.

Each revisit may reveal new observations, revised theories, or growing concern.

If Samantha disappears before investigators recover the notebook, its final entries become especially poignant—not because they contain answers, but because they record the careful reasoning that led her to make one irreversible decision.
