# A-01 — FBI Preliminary Investigation Report

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

FBI Preliminary Investigation Report
## Description

A professionally prepared investigative case file compiled by the Federal Bureau of Investigation following the series of unexplained disappearances in and around Utica, New York.

The report represents the cumulative work of nearly four weeks of investigation conducted prior to the investigators' arrival.

It includes:

Executive case summary.
Chronological timeline of disappearances and recoveries.
Victim profiles.
Family interviews.
Witness interviews.
Search operation reports.
Geographic search maps.
Cell phone records.
Financial and public records.
Employment and educational records.
Local law enforcement reports.
Search-and-rescue documentation.
Medical examinations.
Initial interviews with returned victims.
Photographs.
Evidence logs.

The report is thorough, methodical, and professionally assembled.

It contains no supernatural conclusions.

## Who Possesses It?

The FBI.

Upon assignment to the investigation, the investigators receive unrestricted access to the complete preliminary case file.

Special Agent Elena Alvarez encourages investigators to familiarize themselves with the material before conducting new interviews, emphasizing that duplicate investigative effort wastes valuable time.

## Where Is It Found?

The report is maintained at the FBI Field Command Post established for the investigation.

Investigators receive both printed binders and digital copies shortly after arriving.

It quickly becomes their primary reference document throughout the operation.

## Initial Interpretation

The report immediately establishes two critical facts.

First, the FBI has conducted a competent and comprehensive investigation.

Second, despite that effort, the Bureau has failed to identify a satisfactory explanation for the disappearances.

Nothing in the report suggests investigative negligence.

Instead, investigators encounter an increasingly thorough investigation confronting evidence that refuses to fit conventional explanations.

Several findings immediately stand out.

Timeline of Events

May 6 — Hal Whitaker disappears.

May 15 — David Allen Barlow disappears.

May 27 — Christopher Donnelly disappears.

June 3 — FBI assumes investigative responsibility.

June 7 — Samantha Carter disappears, dramatically increasing media attention and investigative resources.

June 15 — Nathaniel Collins disappears.

June 18 — Samantha Carter returns.

June 19 — First returned victim following FBI involvement is documented. (Timeline subject to reconciliation with witness reporting.)

June 21 — Hal Whitaker returns.

June 24 — Christopher Donnelly returns.

June 25 — Nathaniel Collins returns.

Also on June 25, investigators note a significant anomaly within Collins' historical cellular location data.

This inconsistency ultimately prompts Program interest in the investigation.

## Further Examination
Timeline Reconstruction

The report reconstructs approximately the final ten hours preceding each disappearance.

Although no common destination or suspect emerges, investigators identify recurring themes:

ordinary daily routines,
independent travel,
no evidence of coordinated meetings,
no indication victims knowingly met one another immediately before disappearing.

The disappearances appear unrelated.

Until they no longer do.

Victim Profiles

Each victim receives a detailed investigative profile.

Included are:

employment history,
education,
financial background,
criminal history,
known associates,
family relationships,
medical information,
hobbies,
amateur radio club participation.

The FBI correctly identifies the amateur radio club as the only meaningful connection shared by every victim.

No evidence explains why that connection matters.

Nathaniel Collins' Cellular Data

Nathaniel Collins' historical cellular records become the report's most significant technical finding.

Carrier records indicate his phone traversed geographic areas impossible to reconcile with:

witness testimony,
vehicle location,
E-ZPass records,
surveillance cameras,
or physical travel times.

Multiple independent datasets contradict one another.

Investigators conclude the data cannot all be correct.

No explanation is offered.

Search Operations

Detailed maps document every organized search conducted during each disappearance.

Collectively, hundreds of personnel searched thousands of acres.

Ground teams.

Dogs.

ATVs.

Helicopters.

Thermal imaging.

Volunteer organizations.

Nothing of significance was discovered.

Investigators immediately appreciate the scale of resources already expended.

Victim Interviews

Interviews conducted immediately following each victim's return reveal striking similarities.

Victims consistently report:

little or no memory,
confusion regarding elapsed time,
physical exhaustion,
no obvious signs of coercion,
no coherent explanation.

Investigators note several subtle personality changes but consider them tentative observations rather than established facts.

Educational and Employment Records

The report includes institutional records for Samantha Carter and Nathaniel Collins obtained through subpoena and voluntary cooperation.

These records establish:

Samantha's academic standing,
her engineering coursework,
campus radio involvement,
Nathaniel's employment responsibilities,
campus access records,
communications between the two.

The FBI ultimately concludes their relationship was entirely professional.

What initially appeared to be a promising investigative lead proves unrelated to the disappearances.

Local Law Enforcement

Reports submitted by the New Hartford Police Department, county sheriff's office, and park authorities are included in full.

These documents demonstrate effective cooperation between agencies despite increasing public pressure and media attention.

Investigators inherit an investigation that has been competently managed from its earliest stages.

## Investigation Impact

The FBI Preliminary Investigation Report establishes the operation's baseline reality.

It provides investigators with a comprehensive understanding of everything competent law enforcement has already accomplished.

Rather than asking players to rediscover obvious facts, the report allows the investigation to begin at the point where conventional investigative methods have reached their limits.

This immediately shifts the investigators' objective.

The question is no longer:

"Can we solve the disappearances?"

Instead it becomes:

"What did an excellent investigation fail to notice?"

The report also prevents players from wasting time repeating work already performed.

Searches have been conducted.

Families have been interviewed.

Background investigations completed.

Financial records reviewed.

The investigators are therefore encouraged to pursue new questions rather than duplicate old ones.

## Changes Over Time

The report itself remains static.

Its significance changes as investigators acquire new evidence.

Details initially dismissed as routine—such as amateur radio participation, subtle behavioral changes, or isolated inconsistencies in witness testimony—gradually become central to understanding the operation.

Investigators frequently return to the report, discovering connections they overlooked during their initial review.

It rewards rereading.

## Handler Notes

The FBI Preliminary Investigation Report establishes an important philosophical foundation for the operation:

Competent investigation is not sufficient when confronting the Unnatural.

The FBI is neither incompetent nor dismissive.

The Bureau identifies nearly every meaningful fact available through conventional investigative techniques.

It correctly connects the victims.

Documents timelines.

Conducts exhaustive searches.

Identifies Nate Collins' impossible travel data.

Recognizes the Amateur Radio Club as the sole common link.

Interviews families thoroughly.

Follows every reasonable lead.

And still cannot explain what happened.

This is not a failure of investigative ability.

It is a demonstration of investigative limits.

The investigators' role is therefore not to replace the FBI.

It is to recognize patterns that only become visible when conventional evidence is viewed through a different lens.

In that sense, the Preliminary Report is the operation's ground truth. Every subsequent artifact either expands it, reframes it, or reveals the significance of facts it already contained. It teaches an important lesson that fits beautifully with your campaign's philosophy: the truth was present from the beginning—but it was not yet recognizable.
