# A-05 — Nate Collins SaucerWatch Report

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Artifact: Nate Collins' SaucerWatch Report
## Description

A detailed investigative report authored by Nathaniel Collins and distributed electronically to members of the SaucerWatch online community approximately one week after his return.

Unlike most SaucerWatch submissions, the report avoids speculation and sensational claims.

Instead, Collins presents a structured summary of his own disappearance, focusing on verifiable observations, technical anomalies, and unanswered questions.

The report includes:

A personal timeline of events.
Maps reconstructed from memory.
Amateur radio observations.
Signal analysis.
Environmental descriptions.
Cell phone location discrepancies.
Questions directed to other researchers.
Requests for corroborating experiences.

The writing is analytical, restrained, and often self-critical.

Whenever Collins cannot support a conclusion with evidence, he explicitly says so.

## Who Possesses It?

Nathaniel Collins authored the original report.

It was distributed privately through SaucerWatch shortly before Brooks arrived in Utica.

Brooks maintains a copy within his investigation archive.

Fragments of the report eventually circulate through the broader SaucerWatch community.

## Where Is It Found?

Investigators may obtain the report from:

Nate Collins directly.
Evan Brooks.
Archived SaucerWatch discussions.
Collins' personal computer.

The version preserved by Brooks contains handwritten annotations comparing Collins' observations to earlier disappearance cases.

## Initial Interpretation

The report initially appears to be another well-written UFO account.

Unlike most reports, however, Collins deliberately separates observations from conclusions.

He repeatedly reminds readers:

"I don't know what happened."

"This isn't evidence of aliens."

"I'm only documenting things I can independently verify."

The report identifies several unusual observations:

Persistent radio interference preceding his disappearance.
Significant memory gaps.
Inconsistent cellular location records.
A vague recollection of walking uphill.
Increasing background noise resembling electrical buzzing.
Physical fatigue inconsistent with the distance he remembers traveling.

Rather than explaining these events, Collins asks whether anyone has documented similar experiences.

## Further Examination
Computer Science

The report demonstrates careful handling of digital evidence.

Collins cross-references:

GPS history.
Cellular carrier records.
Personal photographs.
Metadata.
Calendar entries.
Fitness tracker logs.

He documents contradictions without attempting to resolve them.

Several of his observations later prove consistent with evidence independently recovered by the FBI.

Signal Analysis

Collins includes spectral analysis of recorded radio interference gathered before his disappearance.

Although his equipment lacks scientific precision, the report correctly identifies recurring frequency structures later observed by Hal Whitaker and Daniel Kowalski.

He cautiously concludes:

"Whatever this is, it behaves like a signal, not random interference."

Investigative Analysis

Perhaps the report's greatest strength is methodological.

Collins continually distinguishes:

observations,
evidence,
assumptions,
speculation.

He openly revises earlier conclusions when new information contradicts them.

This intellectual honesty earns Brooks' respect and explains why Brooks chooses to travel to Utica.

Comparative Analysis

Brooks' annotations reveal numerous similarities between Collins' account and historical disappearances preserved in his archive.

The annotations remain cautious.

They identify recurring patterns without asserting a common explanation.

Together, Collins and Brooks independently arrive at the same conclusion:

Something unusual is happening.

Neither claims to understand what it is.

## Investigation Impact

Nate Collins' report demonstrates that meaningful investigation can occur outside formal institutions.

While lacking the FBI's resources, Collins compensates through careful documentation, technical competence, and intellectual discipline.

The report also explains why Brooks considers Collins' case fundamentally different from the countless unreliable submissions SaucerWatch receives each year.

It is not the conclusions that impress Brooks.

It is the quality of the evidence.

The report may also encourage investigators to re-examine Collins as an unusually credible witness rather than merely another victim.

## Changes Over Time

The report itself never changes.

Its significance grows as investigators encounter additional evidence.

Initially, Collins' observations appear isolated.

Later discoveries—including Hal's recording, Daniel's transmission, and the Mi-Go operating chamber—confirm that many of Collins' cautious observations were accurate, even though his proposed explanations remained incomplete.

## Handler Notes

Nate Collins' report exists to reinforce an important theme of the operation:

Good investigators can exist outside official institutions.

Collins is neither an FBI agent nor a conspiracy theorist.

He is an intelligent IT professional applying technical skills to an experience he cannot explain.

His report is valuable not because it reaches the correct conclusion—it doesn't—but because it models excellent investigative thinking.

Throughout the report, Collins consistently distinguishes between facts, interpretations, and hypotheses. That discipline makes him one of the operation's most reliable civilian witnesses and explains why Brooks immediately recognizes the report as something different from the noise that usually fills SaucerWatch.

The report also serves as a subtle mirror of the investigators themselves. Like the agents, Collins gathers evidence, acknowledges uncertainty, revises his beliefs when confronted with new information, and resists the temptation to force the unknown into a familiar narrative. In that sense, he succeeds not because he finds answers, but because he asks the right questions. That makes him an ideal civilian counterpart to the Program's own investigative philosophy.
