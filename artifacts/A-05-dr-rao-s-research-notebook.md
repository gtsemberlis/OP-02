# A-05 — Dr. Rao's Research Notebook

## Artifact Type
Handwritten scientific research notebook

## Current Custody
Initially Dr. Ananya Rao. Final custody depends on investigator decisions; the Program should retain either the original or a complete archival copy.

## Current Location
Initially L-03 — Dr. Rao's Office; later secured according to the operation's outcome.

## First Appearance
S-03 — Dr. Ananya Rao

## Description
A series of carefully maintained notebooks containing observations, hypotheses, diagrams, citations, failed explanations, blank spaces, and cross-references concerning the phenomenon.

## Presentation Notes
The notebook should feel used, disciplined, and human. Handwriting is precise but increasingly dense. Unsupported conclusions are crossed out or left blank rather than asserted.

## Investigator Access Conditions
Rao may share selected portions through rapport and professional trust. Full access follows the Collector incident, voluntary transfer, or Program seizure.

## Canonical Content
Recurring material includes:

- Cross-modal persistence diagrams.
- Historical incident comparisons.
- Bird-avoidance observations.
- Notes on changes in migratory behavior around active Alexandria equipment.
- Sketches of large ray-like forms and smaller plankton-like forms.
- Questions about whether observation changes biological proximity to perception.
- Explicit separation of observation, pattern, hypothesis, and conclusion.
- Numerous unresolved questions.

## Operational Significance
A-05 is the operation's central scientific artifact. It demonstrates that disciplined inquiry, not instability, led Rao toward the correct hypothesis.

## Preservation Notes
If the original remains with Rao, the Program retains a complete copy. Any missing pages or Collector access should be documented in the campaign record.
