# A-02 — Hal Whitaker Radio Recording

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

HAL WHITAKER RADIO RECORDING
## Description
A twenty-second digital audio recording accompanied by a frequency analysis spectrogram generated by Hal Whitaker on June 26 during routine amateur radio monitoring.
The recording contains no discernible speech or conventional modulation.
Instead, a narrow high-frequency signal repeatedly pulses across an otherwise quiet portion of the spectrum.
The pulses occur at irregular intervals.
They do not resemble Morse code.
They do not exhibit recognizable digital encoding.
They are neither random noise nor obviously structured communication.
The accompanying spectrogram shows a remarkably clean signal occupying a frequency range where Hal would not normally expect persistent transmissions.
Hal has monitored this portion of the spectrum for years.
He has never encountered anything comparable.

## Who Possesses It?
Hal Whitaker.
He willingly provides both the recording and original spectrogram after establishing trust with the investigators.
He is genuinely interested in hearing alternative explanations.

## Where Is It Found?
Hal Whitaker's residence.
Original files remain on his amateur radio workstation.
Printed spectrograms may also be available.

## Initial Interpretation
Even without specialized knowledge, investigators immediately recognize several unusual characteristics.
The signal is unusually clean.
There is no audible voice.
No recognizable modulation.
No obvious terrestrial source.
The recording is interesting.
It is not yet extraordinary.
At this stage, it could still represent unfamiliar equipment, environmental interference, or an undocumented transmission.

## Further Examination
I actually like organizing this by discipline rather than skill rolls.
That makes the artifact reward multiple investigator backgrounds.
SIGINT / Military Communications
Recognizes the occupied band is inconsistent with known military allocations.
No familiar military modulation is present.
It does not resemble encrypted tactical communications, radar, telemetry, or satellite downlinks.
The signal is simply "wrong."

Electronics Engineering
Determines the signal-to-noise ratio is unexpectedly high for the equipment Hal used.
Given the modest receiving antenna, the transmitter is likely either:
considerably more powerful than expected,
or
significantly closer than initially assumed.
Either conclusion deserves further investigation.

Physics
Confirms the propagation characteristics are inconsistent with common atmospheric reflections or sporadic radio phenomena.
Nothing rules out an unknown terrestrial transmitter.
But ordinary propagation does not comfortably explain the recording.

Mathematics / Signal Processing
Attempts statistical analysis of pulse intervals.
The spacing is demonstrably non-random.
Yet no conventional encoding algorithm produces meaningful structure.
The investigators can confidently conclude:
Someone—or something—is generating deliberate pulses.
They simply cannot determine the encoding scheme.

Computer Science
Digital filtering and Fourier analysis remove equipment artifacts.
The signal persists.
It is not an artifact of Hal's recording software.

Amateur Radio / Electrical Repair
Recognizes Hal's equipment was correctly calibrated.
No obvious operator error exists.
His station logs also indicate excellent receiving conditions that evening.

Navigation / Surveying
Using known antenna characteristics and approximate field strength, investigators may estimate an approximate radius within which the transmitter could plausibly exist.
This does not reveal the chamber.
It merely constrains the search area.

I would avoid allowing one successful roll to produce coordinates.
Instead, each discipline removes uncertainty.

## Relationships
This is where I think the artifact really shines.
Nate Collins Evidence Package
Nate's recordings independently detect the same signal.
Comparison confirms the phenomenon is persistent rather than isolated.

Daniel Kowalski Ranger Recording
Daniel unknowingly records subtle changes in the signal while moving through different terrain.
Relative signal strength may vary.

Samantha Carter Field Journal
Samantha's hiking observations identify locations where radio behavior seemed "different."
These observations become meaningful only after investigators compare them with Hal's recording.

Brooks Investigation Report
Brooks has independently collected additional amateur radio reports.
Most are useless.
A few corroborate Hal's observations.

Hogsback State Forest
Combined with terrain analysis, investigators can gradually eliminate impossible transmission locations.
Notice the wording.
Not locate.
Eliminate.
That distinction reinforces your earlier navigation principle.

## Investigation Impact
The recording establishes one important operational fact.
There is an objectively measurable phenomenon associated with Hogsback State Forest.
It cannot yet be explained.
It can, however, be studied.
The recording also begins shifting the investigation away from psychology and toward physical evidence.
The disappearances are no longer supported solely by witness testimony.
The environment itself has begun producing observable anomalies.

## Changes Over Time
The recording itself never changes.
Its meaning does.
Initially it appears to be an isolated curiosity.
As investigators acquire additional artifacts, it gradually becomes one vertex of a larger network of corroborating evidence.
By the end of the operation, investigators may recognize it as one of the earliest objective indicators pointing toward the operating chamber.

## Handler Notes
The recording is intentionally insufficient on its own.
No combination of examinations should produce the location of the Mi-Go chamber.
Instead, every successful analysis should narrow uncertainty.
The recording rewards technical investigators by allowing them to progressively constrain the search space rather than unlocking the solution.
This directly supports the operation's navigation philosophy: the Mi-Go chamber is not discovered by following a hidden clue, but by synthesizing many independent observations into a coherent model of the landscape.
