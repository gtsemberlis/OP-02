# H-06 — Owen Walker's Drawing

## Artifact Type
Civilian drawing / observational artifact

## Current Custody
Original remains with Sarah and Owen Walker unless voluntarily surrendered. Program retains a high-resolution photographic copy.

## Current Location
Initially L-04 — NTSB Field Office; original later returns with the Walker family.

## First Appearance
S-06 — Witness Interviews

## Description
A crayon drawing made by seven-year-old Owen Walker on plain white office paper while adults speak nearby.

The drawing depicts:

- A slightly wonky passenger airplane.
- An asymmetrical control tower.
- Inconsistent clouds.
- Two large manta-ray-like shapes, one with a different wing shape from the other.
- Crayon coloring outside the lines.

## Presentation Notes
The drawing must look ordinary, imperfect, and age-appropriate. It is not prophetic art. The manta-ray shapes receive no special dramatic emphasis.

## Investigator Access Conditions
Investigators must notice it naturally. Owen does not present it as evidence, and Karen Delgado and Sarah Walker do not assign it significance.

## Canonical Content
If asked gently, Owen treats every element as part of what he remembers. He does not understand that the ray-like forms are unusual or invisible to most adults.

## Operational Significance
H-06 rewards observation rather than interrogation. It provides independent, unfiltered corroboration without being necessary to complete the investigation.

## Preservation Notes
The original should not be seized without compelling reason. A respectful photographic copy is sufficient for the Program archive.
