# A-11 — Mi-Go Brain Cylinder

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Artifact: Mi-Go Brain Cylinder
## Description

A transparent cylindrical vessel approximately sixty centimeters tall and twenty centimeters in diameter.

The cylinder is constructed from an unknown material resembling glass while remaining noticeably warmer than the surrounding environment.

No seams, fasteners, or visible manufacturing marks are present.

Inside, suspended within a perfectly clear fluid, rests a living human brain.

Delicate black and silver filaments emerge from the base of the cylinder, disappearing into the surrounding machinery.

The brain appears remarkably healthy.

No blood is visible.

No obvious surgical trauma can be identified.

Depending upon the circumstances of the investigation, the brain belongs to either:

Daniel Kowalski, or
Samantha Carter.

The cylinder bears no labels.

To the Mi-Go, it appears to be merely one component within a much larger process.

## Who Possesses It?

The Mi-Go.

The cylinder remains within the operating chamber until investigators encounter it.

Removing the cylinder is physically possible, though doing so immediately raises difficult medical, ethical, and operational questions.

## Where Is It Found?

The cylinder occupies a recessed mounting point within the Mi-Go operating chamber.

It is connected to surrounding equipment through unfamiliar biological and mechanical interfaces.

If the chamber is active, the cylinder is one of several pieces of equipment currently in use.

## Initial Interpretation

The cylinder immediately communicates one horrifying fact.

The brain is alive.

The investigators may initially refuse to believe what they are seeing.

Closer examination confirms the impossible.

The occupant has not been killed.

Nor are they obviously conscious.

Whatever process the Mi-Go employ appears intended to preserve neurological function rather than destroy it.

The realization transforms the investigation.

Missing persons have become recoverable victims.

Time suddenly matters.

## Further Examination
Medicine

The brain displays every outward indication of remaining biologically viable.

Perfusion appears continuous.

No significant tissue deterioration is visible.

The preservation medium does not resemble blood, saline, or any known medical solution.

No human technology could plausibly sustain isolated neural tissue under these conditions.

Biology

The supporting filaments appear neither purely biological nor entirely mechanical.

They interface with the brain at remarkable precision.

No investigator can confidently identify where living tissue ends and artificial structures begin.

Neuroscience

Electrical activity appears ongoing.

Whether this activity represents consciousness, dreaming, memory, or simple neurological maintenance cannot be determined.

The investigators cannot answer the most important question.

Is the victim aware?

Engineering

The cylinder itself displays manufacturing tolerances beyond conventional human capability.

No power source is visible.

No pumps.

No circulation system.

Yet the preservation environment remains perfectly stable.

## Relationships

The cylinder fundamentally recontextualizes numerous earlier discoveries.

It confirms that:

the disappearances are deliberate,
the Mi-Go possess advanced neurological technology,
victims remain biologically valuable after abduction.

The artifact directly connects to:

Daniel Kowalski's disappearance,
Samantha Carter's disappearance,
Brooks' historical cases,
recovered witness testimony describing missing time,
the operating chamber itself.

It also raises an unsettling possibility.

If historical victims never returned...

perhaps some cylinders were never emptied.

## Investigation Impact

The Brain Cylinder completely changes the investigators' priorities.

Until this moment, the investigation has focused on understanding what happened.

The cylinder reframes the operation as an immediate rescue.

Containment remains important.

So does understanding the Mi-Go.

But the investigators now possess direct evidence that at least one missing person may still be recoverable.

The artifact also presents an immediate ethical dilemma.

Should the cylinder be removed?

Should the process be interrupted?

Should the victim be left in place until the surrounding machinery is understood?

No available evidence clearly identifies the safest course of action.

Every option carries unknown risks.

## Changes Over Time

The cylinder itself changes very little.

The occupant's identity changes depending upon the state of the investigation.

If investigators reach the chamber before Samantha disappears, the cylinder contains Daniel Kowalski.

If Samantha has already returned to the forest and been taken, the cylinder instead contains Samantha Carter.

The Mi-Go appear entirely unconcerned by which human occupies the apparatus.

The process continues uninterrupted.

## Handler Notes

The Brain Cylinder exists to transform the emotional stakes of the operation.

Until this point, the disappearances remain largely abstract.

The investigators pursue clues.

Interview witnesses.

Analyze evidence.

The cylinder forces those abstractions to become immediate and personal.

Importantly, the cylinder should never become an object of gore.

The Mi-Go are not torturing their captives.

Nor are they collecting trophies.

From their perspective, this is simply a sophisticated neurological procedure.

That indifference is far more disturbing than cruelty.

Finally, the cylinder reinforces one of the operation's central philosophical themes:

The Mi-Go are not evil because they delight in suffering.

They are terrifying because human concepts such as bodily integrity, identity, and personhood appear irrelevant to them.

To the investigators, the cylinder contains a person.

To the Mi-Go, it contains a functioning brain.

The difference between those two perspectives is the true source of horror.
