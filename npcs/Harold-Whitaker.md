# PER-02 — Harold “Hal” Whitaker

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

> Status: LOCKED CANONICAL NPC

Victim 2 — Harold "Hal" Whitaker
Basic Information

Full Name: Harold James Whitaker

Known As: "Hal"

Age: 71

Occupation: Retired Electrical Engineer

Residence: New Hartford, New York

Marital Status: Widower

Family:

Wife (deceased): Margaret Whitaker (d. 2019)
Daughter: Rebecca Lawson (44), High School English Teacher
Son-in-law: Daniel Lawson (46), Accountant

Licensed Amateur Radio Operator (Extra Class)

Club member for over thirty years.

## Public Record

Harold Whitaker was born in Rome, New York.

Graduated from the State University of New York at Buffalo with a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering.

Worked for Niagara Mohawk Power Corporation (later National Grid) for thirty-eight years.

Career focused on transmission protection systems, substations, and electrical reliability.

Held several patents related to protective relay circuitry during the 1980s and early 1990s.

Retired at age sixty-six.

No criminal record.

No civil litigation.

Financially secure.

Excellent credit.

Long history of volunteer work with local STEM education initiatives and amateur radio emergency communications.

His wife Margaret died after a prolonged battle with ovarian cancer.

Following her death, Whitaker became increasingly active in amateur radio.

## Physical Description

Height: 5'10"

Weight: 168 lbs

Lean build.

White hair.

Neatly trimmed beard.

Gray-blue eyes.

Usually wears pressed button-down shirts, khaki trousers, and an old utility jacket regardless of season.

Walks with a slight stiffness from arthritis in both knees.

Always wears an analog wristwatch.

Reading glasses perpetually hang from a cord around his neck.

## Biography

Harold spent nearly four decades solving problems most people never noticed.

Electrical grids were expected to work.

When they did, nobody thought about the engineers responsible.

Coworkers remember him as meticulous, patient, and deeply committed to engineering ethics.

He mentored dozens of younger engineers throughout his career.

After retirement he never stopped building things.

His basement workshop gradually transformed into an electronics laboratory.

The amateur radio club increasingly became both an intellectual outlet and a social anchor following his wife's death.

Members often describe him as "the professor," despite never having taught professionally.

## Private Context

Margaret's death fundamentally changed Harold.

He never became depressed enough to concern his family.

Instead, life quietly narrowed.

He stopped traveling.

Stopped attending concerts.

Stopped hosting holidays.

The radio club became his primary remaining social connection.

His greatest private fear is not death.

It's irrelevance.

He worries that his accumulated knowledge will simply disappear when he does.

He has begun quietly organizing decades of engineering notebooks in hopes someone may someday find them useful.

Cognitive Signature

Harold naturally seeks first principles.

He dislikes explanations that rely on coincidence.

He mentally decomposes complex systems into smaller interacting components.

Excellent long-term memory.

Exceptional mathematical intuition.

Deliberate thinker.

Will reconsider conclusions when presented with stronger evidence.

Values intellectual honesty above being correct.

## Communication Style

Measured.

Patient.

Frequently pauses before answering.

Prefers complete explanations over short answers.

Often sketches diagrams on nearby paper while speaking.

Rarely raises his voice.

Never intentionally talks down to others, although his technical vocabulary occasionally overwhelms listeners.

## Under Pressure

Becomes more analytical.

Narrows attention toward observable facts.

Actively suppresses speculation.

If others panic, Harold instinctively assumes the role of educator, slowing conversations and organizing information into manageable pieces.

His calmness is reassuring but can sometimes be mistaken for emotional detachment.

## Investigative Utility

Harold maintained detailed engineering notebooks throughout both his professional career and retirement.

His personal journals contain:

propagation observations
antenna performance measurements
weather correlations
equipment modifications
unusual radio interference
maintenance logs

Unlike Chris's practical workshop notes, Harold's journals include hypotheses, calculations, and diagrams.

Investigators willing to spend time reading them may discover recurring anomalies that Harold himself dismissed as statistical curiosities rather than evidence of anything paranormal.

His home computer also contains decades of carefully archived digital logs with impeccable organization.

Post-Incident Changes

Harold returns physically healthy.

Medical examination finds no abnormalities.

The changes emerge gradually over several days.

Most noticeably:

He sleeps through the night.

For nearly six years after Margaret's death, Harold averaged only three to four hours of sleep.

Following his return, he begins sleeping seven to eight uninterrupted hours every night.

His daughter initially celebrates the change.

She tells investigators:

"I haven't seen him look this rested since Mom was alive."

Additional observations include:

Reduced chronic knee pain.
Increased appetite.
Renewed interest in old hobbies.
More frequent laughter.

Harold himself acknowledges the changes without enthusiasm.

When asked how he feels, he quietly says:

"Better."

After a long pause, he adds:

"That's what concerns me."

When investigators ask why, he answers with characteristic precision:

"Grief doesn't simply disappear. If mine has... then something removed it."

That single statement neatly captures Harold's character. Unlike the others, he recognizes that the improvements are genuine—but as an engineer, he refuses to accept an unexplained result without questioning the process that produced it. He may become the first returned victim to suspect that something profound happened to him, even if he cannot explain what it was.
