**Residence:** Madison, Wisconsin  
**Seat:** 12F, starboard window.

## Physical Description
An attentive man in his early thirties who becomes animated when discussing aviation but visibly reins himself in before overstating a conclusion.

## Operational Role
Recognizes changes in flight profile and procedural cadence that most passengers would overlook.

## Biography
A software engineer active in civilian aviation photography and aircraft-tracking communities. He has no pilot certification, ATC training, or professional aviation experience.

## Private Context
He worries investigators will dismiss him as an internet hobbyist and therefore self-edits aggressively.

## Cognitive Signature
### Sequence Recognition
His governing question is: “What changed from the normal pattern?”

## Communication Style
Friendly, qualified, and precise about the difference between observation and inference.

## Under Pressure
Becomes quieter and retreats to facts rather than arguing for his interpretation.

## Investigative Utility
Notes the late engine-power increase, decisive maneuver, changed cadence, and absence of a visible hazard.

## Operational Knowledge
Knows civilian aviation terminology unevenly and cannot identify what ATC, the pilots, or automated systems perceived.

## Post-Operation Status
Available for follow-up and likely to continue privately reviewing public flight data.
