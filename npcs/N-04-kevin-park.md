# N-04 — Kevin Park

## Affiliation
Alexandria Aviation Systems

## Position / Occupation
Lead Systems Engineer

## Primary Location
L-02 — Alexandria Aviation Systems Headquarters

## Current Status
Active and responsible for technical validation and intrusion-system analysis.

## Personnel Record
**Full Name:** Kevin Daniel Park  
**Age:** 34  
**Education:** B.S., Computer Engineering — Georgia Tech; M.S., Machine Learning — Carnegie Mellon  
**Previous Employment:** Lockheed Martin Advanced Systems; Aurora Flight Sciences; Alexandria Aviation Systems  
**Professional Specialties:** Machine-learning infrastructure, sensor integration, distributed systems, real-time data processing, model validation  
**Professional Reputation:** An exceptional systems engineer known for finding why complex systems fail.  
**Selected Projects:** Alexandria Multi-Modal Sensor Fusion Platform; Distributed Confidence Verification Pipeline; Adaptive Runway Obstruction Detection Framework; Real-Time Model Validation Architecture  
**Recognition:** Alexandria Technical Excellence Award; IEEE Intelligent Systems engineering recognition  
**Residence:** Chicago  
**Family:** Single; close to an older sister who is an emergency physician in Milwaukee.

## Physical Description
Kevin is a slim man in his mid-thirties with restless energy, black hair repeatedly pushed out of place, thin rectangular glasses, and practical Alexandria work clothes. He sketches diagrams while thinking. The first impression is an engineer happiest beside a server rack rather than in a conference room.

## Operational Role
Kevin explains the platform, validates performance, eliminates conventional failures, and reconstructs the technical aspects of the Alexandria intrusion.

## Biography
Kevin built his career eliminating uncertainty through engineering. His first instinct is to prove the platform wrong. Only after eliminating software, hardware, synchronization, calibration, and corruption failures does he accept the boundary of engineering explanation.

## Private Context
He worries that accepting extraordinary explanations prematurely would undermine disciplined practice, but refuses to ignore evidence that violates expectation.

## Cognitive Signature
### Engineering Pragmatism
His governing question is: “How could this system produce that result?”

## Communication Style
Conversational and technically fluent. He thinks aloud, sketches constantly, and slows down when explaining systems to non-engineers.

## Under Pressure
Runs additional diagnostics, builds validation scripts, checks logs, and narrows uncertainty. The fact that intruders ignored Alexandria's technical infrastructure is a critical observation.

## Investigative Utility
Provides engineering context and shows that extraordinary conclusions become stronger after responsible attempts to disprove them.

## Relationships
**Mercer:** Trusts him to protect engineering integrity.  
**Rao:** Different methods, deep mutual respect.  
**Ortiz:** Works closely where infrastructure meets systems.  
**Investigators:** Respects thoughtful questions; impatient with unsupported speculation.

## Operational Knowledge
**Confirmed:** Platform architecture, validation history, technical integrity, and what systems the intruders did or did not access.  
**Suspects:** Conventional intrusion or espionage until physical evidence contradicts it.  
**Unknown:** The phenomenon's nature and the Collectors' purpose.

## Post-Operation Status
Remains with Alexandria unless the company is shut down. He supports continued controlled investigation if evidence and safety protocols justify it.

## Operational Notes
Never portray Kevin as dismissive, socially awkward, or anti-science. He is empirical, personable, and collaborative.

## Mechanical Reference
**Profession:** Systems Engineer  
**Attributes:** High INT and EDU; above-average POW.  
**Key Skills:** Computer Science, Science (Engineering), Electronics, SIGINT, Search, Alertness, Craft (Programming), Bureaucracy.  
**Combat:** Minimal.
