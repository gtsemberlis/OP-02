# PER-15 — Michael and Laura Carter

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

> Status: LOCKED CANONICAL

Michael & Laura Carter
Basic Information

Michael Carter

Age: 51

Occupation: Electrical Engineer

Laura Carter

Age: 49

Occupation: Public Librarian

Relationship: Parents of Samantha Carter

Residence: New Hartford, New York

Married for twenty-six years.

Samantha is their only child.

Public Overview

Michael and Laura represent two very different responses to uncertainty.

Michael believes every problem has an explanation waiting to be discovered.

Laura understands that some questions may never be answered.

They love each other deeply, but Samantha's disappearance has amplified these differences.

Michael searches.

Laura mourns.

Both desperately want answers.

Personality
Michael

Analytical.

Methodical.

Quietly stubborn.

Keeps notebooks documenting every interaction with investigators.

Has reconstructed Samantha's final weeks dozens of times looking for something everyone else missed.

Laura

Warm.

Patient.

Deeply empathetic.

The emotional center of the family.

She notices people before facts.

She often answers questions by describing who Samantha was rather than what she did.

Relationship with Samantha

Michael encouraged Samantha's interest in engineering from childhood.

They built radios together.

Repaired old electronics.

Visited amateur radio field days.

He proudly describes her as "smarter than I ever was."

Laura nurtured Samantha's curiosity in different ways.

Books.

Music.

Museums.

Astronomy nights.

She admired Samantha's determination to understand the world.

Both parents describe Samantha as independent without ever being rebellious.

Interview Guide
What They Know
Samantha became interested in amateur radio during high school.
Hal Whitaker became an important mentor.
Nate Collins encouraged her technical interests.
Chris Donnelly occasionally helped with equipment repairs.
Samantha spent increasing amounts of time with the amateur radio community during the year before disappearing.

Neither parent ever considered those relationships inappropriate.

They viewed them as exactly the kind of mentors every curious young person deserves.

What They Don't Know

They have no knowledge of:

Delta Green.
Mi-Go.
the Unnatural.
classified investigations.
hidden research.
secret organizations.

They believe Samantha was simply surrounded by unusually kind adults who shared her interests.

Behavioral Observations

A successful HUMINT or Psychology roll reveals:

Michael and Laura describe Samantha's behavioral changes very differently.

Michael notices objective changes.

Laura notices emotional ones.

Neither realizes they are describing the same phenomenon.

Michael Says

"She started making eye contact."

He immediately looks embarrassed.

"That sounds strange."

"I just mean..."

"She always looked at people before."

"Now she looked through conversations instead of around them."

Laura Says

"She stopped apologizing."

When asked what she means:

"She used to apologize for everything."

"Interrupting."

"Talking too much."

"Talking too little."

"Existing."

A long silence.

"Then one day..."

"She didn't anymore."

If Asked About Samantha's Personality

Michael describes Samantha as exceptionally intelligent.

Laura immediately adds:

"And funny."

Michael smiles.

"Yes."

Another pause.

"People forgot that."

This small exchange tells investigators far more about Samantha than either answer alone.

If Asked About Changes Before Her Disappearance

Michael initially frames the changes positively.

More confident.

More outgoing.

Less anxious.

Laura quietly interrupts.

"Not confident."

Everyone turns toward her.

"Comfortable."

She cannot explain why those words feel different.

But they do.

If Asked About Nate Collins

Both parents speak warmly of Nate.

Michael respected his technical knowledge.

Laura appreciated how naturally he encouraged Samantha without talking down to her.

Neither parent ever worried about their relationship.

Laura says:

"He treated her like she belonged."

Investigative Value

Michael and Laura provide investigators with:

Samantha's developmental history.
Family background.
Academic interests.
Additional confirmation of her pre-disappearance behavioral changes.
Strong evidence that her relationships with older members of the amateur radio club were healthy mentorships rather than sources of exploitation.

Together, they also reinforce the recurring pattern that every witness describes the same transformation from a different perspective.

## Handler Notes

Michael and Laura should almost always be portrayed together.

They naturally alternate between facts and feelings.

Michael answers questions.

Laura answers people.

Neither dominates the conversation, and they frequently complete each other's thoughts after decades of marriage.

One of the interview's most memorable moments should be the distinction they unconsciously make between confidence and comfort. Michael interprets Samantha's transformation as growing self-assurance, while Laura senses something subtler: it wasn't that Samantha learned to overcome her insecurities—it was as if those insecurities had simply ceased to exist.

This distinction quietly foreshadows one of Episode 2's central revelations. The victims did not heal through growth or experience. Something external altered the emotional landscape of their minds. The Carters cannot articulate that truth, but together they become the first witnesses to describe it with remarkable clarity.
