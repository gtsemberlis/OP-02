# N-10 — Robert “Bob” Gallagher

## Affiliation
Civilian

## Position / Occupation
Retired High School History Teacher

## Primary Location
L-01 — Chicago O'Hare International Airport / Local Hospital

## Current Status
Injured survivor.

## Personnel Record
**Full Name:** Robert Michael Gallagher  
**Age:** 61  
**Residence:** Madison, Wisconsin  
**Travel Context:** Returning after visiting his daughter and newborn grandson.

## Physical Description
A slightly stocky man in his early sixties with thinning gray hair, a neat mustache, bruising, and one arm in a sling. He appears ordinary and determined to be helpful.

## Operational Role
Preserves the immediate emotional and sensory reality of the accident.

## Biography
A patient, well-liked retired teacher with no special aviation expertise. The incident is the most frightening experience of his life.

## Cognitive Signature
### Immediate Experience
His governing question is: “What happened to us?” Memory is organized around sensation, fear, faces, and acts of kindness.

## Communication Style
Slow, thoughtful, and careful to distinguish “I remember” from “I think.”

## Investigative Utility
Demonstrates that emotional truth and factual uncertainty can coexist.

## Operational Notes
Never portray Gallagher as confused, unreliable, or melodramatic. His memory is uneven because of trauma, not dishonesty.
