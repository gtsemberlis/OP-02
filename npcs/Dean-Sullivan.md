Full Name: Dr. Thomas Edward Sullivan

Employer: Mohawk Valley Community College

Residence: Clinton, New York

Marital Status: Married

Two adult children.

## Public Record

Thomas Sullivan was born in Syracuse, New York.

He earned a Bachelor's Degree in Mechanical Engineering before completing a Master's Degree in Higher Education Administration and a Doctorate in Educational Leadership.

His career spans more than forty years in higher education.

Previous appointments include:

Engineering faculty member
Department Chair
Associate Dean
Dean of Engineering at a regional state university

After formally retiring, he accepted a part-time dean position at Mohawk Valley Community College.

Colleagues describe the move as less a second career than an opportunity to work directly with students again.

He remains active in regional STEM outreach and workforce development initiatives.

No disciplinary history.

Widely respected throughout the SUNY system.

## Physical Description

Height: 6'0"

Weight: 195 lbs

Lean build.

White hair, neatly combed.

Blue-gray eyes.

Usually wears sport coats, dress shirts, and khakis rather than formal suits.

Wire-rim glasses.

Moves with the relaxed confidence of someone comfortable in almost any academic setting.

Frequently carries a leather portfolio filled with handwritten notes despite having access to every modern digital tool.

## Biography

Thomas spent most of his career building engineering programs and mentoring faculty.

Over time he discovered he missed interacting with students.

Administrative success had gradually pulled him further from the classroom.

Retirement offered an unexpected opportunity.

When Mohawk Valley Community College advertised for a dean willing to mentor faculty while helping expand technical programs, Thomas accepted almost immediately.

He believes community colleges perform some of the most important educational work in America.

He often says:

"Universities create specialists."

"Community colleges create opportunities."

## Private Context

Thomas quietly worries that higher education increasingly values metrics over mentorship.

He knows graduation rates.

Retention percentages.

Transfer statistics.

He also knows those numbers rarely capture the student who simply needed one professor to believe they belonged.

He intentionally learns students' names.

Not all of them.

But enough that people are surprised when he remembers them months later.

Samantha Carter is one of those students.

Nate Collins is one of those employees.

Their disappearances feel deeply personal.

Cognitive Signature

Broad systems thinker.

Excellent at recognizing long-term institutional patterns.

Strong consensus builder.

Naturally considers multiple stakeholders before making decisions.

Less interested in being right than in finding sustainable solutions.

Prefers mentoring over directing.

## Communication Style

Warm.

Patient.

Thoughtful.

Frequently answers questions with stories drawn from decades in education.

Rarely speaks in absolutes.

Excellent at making nervous students feel comfortable.

Comfortable with silence during conversations.

## Under Pressure

Initially gathers information quietly.

Avoids emotional reactions in public.

Privately experiences significant stress but rarely displays it.

Focuses on protecting students, faculty, and institutional stability simultaneously.

If forced to choose, he places people ahead of reputation.

Operational & Mechanical Notes

Dean Sullivan becomes the investigators' principal contact within the college.

He authorizes access to:

student records (through appropriate legal process)
employment files
campus facilities
faculty interviews

He never attempts to obstruct the investigation.

His only concern is ensuring that students and faculty are treated fairly while the investigation proceeds.

He is one of the first people to recognize that the disappearances are beginning to affect campus morale in ways statistics cannot measure.

Design Notes

Dean Sullivan represents the institution of education in the operation. Unlike Chief Patrick O'Connor, who protects a town, or Special Agent Elena Alvarez, who protects the integrity of an investigation, Thomas Sullivan's instinct is to protect the people entrusted to his institution.

He also serves an important narrative function by connecting Nate Collins and Samantha Carter through entirely ordinary means. He can speak credibly about their interactions because he understands the culture of the engineering program: talented students naturally sought out knowledgeable staff, and Nate naturally mentored those who showed genuine curiosity. His testimony reinforces that their relationship was professional, healthy, and precisely the kind of mentorship a college hopes to foster.

Finally, Thomas provides a perspective none of the other NPCs can. When he speaks about Samantha and Nate, he doesn't describe evidence or procedures—he describes potential. Their disappearances are not just missing-person cases to him; they are interruptions of lives that were still in the process of becoming. That makes him a quiet but emotionally resonant presence in the investigation.
