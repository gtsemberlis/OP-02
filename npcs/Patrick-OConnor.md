## Public Record

Michael O'Connor was born in Utica, New York, and raised in New Hartford.

After graduating from high school, he attended Mohawk Valley Community College before earning a Bachelor's Degree in Criminal Justice from SUNY Polytechnic Institute.

He joined the New Hartford Police Department at twenty-two and has served continuously for nearly twenty-five years.

Assignments have included:

Patrol
Criminal Investigations
Detective Division
Patrol Lieutenant
Deputy Chief
Chief of Police

He is a graduate of the FBI National Academy and regularly participates in regional law enforcement task forces.

No disciplinary actions.

Well respected by neighboring agencies.

Frequently speaks at community events and local schools.

## Physical Description

Height: 5'11"

Weight: 205 lbs

Broad-shouldered with a solid build that reflects years of physical work more than regular exercise.

Short brown hair beginning to gray at the temples.

Blue eyes.

Keeps a neatly trimmed beard.

Usually wears the department's Class B uniform unless circumstances require otherwise.

Carries himself with quiet confidence rather than command presence.

Looks like someone who has spent decades putting people at ease during their worst days.

## Biography

Michael O'Connor never intended to become Chief.

He enjoyed patrol work and later investigations, preferring direct service over administration.

Promotion came gradually as respected supervisors retired.

He accepted each promotion reluctantly but viewed leadership as another form of public service.

As Chief, he emphasizes professionalism, transparency, and community relationships.

His officers know him as someone who expects competence but protects his people when criticism is unfair.

He still responds personally to major incidents whenever practical.

The string of disappearances has become the defining challenge of his career.

## Private Context

Michael carries every unsolved case personally.

He keeps copies of photographs from unresolved investigations in a drawer of his desk.

Not as evidence.

As reminders.

He believes every missing person deserves someone who refuses to forget them.

By the time the fifth disappearance occurs, he is sleeping poorly and privately questioning whether his department has overlooked something obvious.

He would never admit this to his officers.

Cognitive Signature

Methodical.

Evidence-driven.

Excellent interviewer.

Strong intuition built upon decades of practical experience rather than instinct alone.

Naturally prioritizes victim welfare over arrest statistics.

Very good at recognizing when witnesses are withholding information.

Less comfortable evaluating highly technical evidence.

## Communication Style

Measured.

Professional.

Speaks plainly.

Avoids law enforcement jargon whenever civilians are present.

Listens more than he speaks.

Rarely interrupts.

When he asks a question, people generally feel he genuinely wants the answer.

## Under Pressure

Becomes calmer.

Narrows his focus.

Delegates effectively.

Protects his personnel from unnecessary distractions.

When overwhelmed by uncertainty, he increases organization rather than urgency.

He is slow to embrace extraordinary explanations, but once evidence becomes overwhelming, he adjusts without defensiveness.

Operational & Mechanical Notes

Chief O'Connor becomes the investigators' principal local liaison.

He grants access to:

evidence storage
interview rooms
search maps
incident command documentation
departmental records
local officers familiar with each disappearance

He initially assumes the investigators are pursuing a sophisticated criminal offender or organized group.

He never obstructs the investigation.

Instead, he repeatedly asks what resources his department can provide.

If investigators demonstrate professionalism, he becomes one of their strongest allies.

Design Notes

Chief O'Connor is intentionally written as a good police chief, not a dramatic obstacle. His function is to reinforce the grounded realism of the investigation. The Program does not succeed because local law enforcement is incompetent; it succeeds because the evidence eventually points beyond the framework that ordinary policing is designed to address.

His greatest strength—and his greatest limitation—is his unwavering belief that every mystery has a human explanation. That conviction has served him well throughout his career. Episode 2 is the first case that asks him to confront the possibility that it may not be enough. I think that makes his eventual interactions with the Delta Green Friendly especially compelling: one man committed to protecting his town, the other committed to protecting the world, both trying to solve the same case from fundamentally different assumptions.
