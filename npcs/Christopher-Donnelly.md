## Background

Michael Patrick Donnelly was born in Utica, New York.

Raised in Marcy by a machinist father and elementary school teacher.

Graduated Whitesboro High School.

Completed Mohawk Valley Community College's Precision Machining Technology program.

Has worked continuously in advanced manufacturing for over twenty years.

Currently employed by a regional aerospace and precision manufacturing supplier producing high-tolerance components for commercial and defense contracts.

No military service.

No criminal history.

No civil litigation outside routine property records.

Excellent credit.

No significant financial distress.

Homeowner.

Married eighteen years.

No history of domestic disturbances.

Licensed Amateur Radio Operator for approximately nine years.

Volunteer participant during county emergency communications exercises.

## Biography

Friends and coworkers consistently describe Donnelly as dependable, patient, and quiet.

He rarely speaks unless he believes he has something useful to contribute.

Known among coworkers for solving difficult machining problems without seeking recognition.

Outside work, spends much of his free time in a detached garage workshop restoring vintage machine tools, fabricating amateur radio equipment, and woodworking.

Has never sought leadership positions within either his workplace or amateur radio club.

Widely respected for practical expertise.

## Private Context

Mike finds comfort in systems that behave predictably.

Machines make sense.

People often don't.

His workshop is less a hobby than a refuge.

When overwhelmed, he disappears into projects.

His marriage is healthy but quiet.

Jennifer understands this instinct and generally leaves him alone when he's building something.

His greatest private concern is not money or work.

It's becoming emotionally distant from his children as they grow older.

He worries he is becoming the kind of father who is present without truly being engaged.

He has never spoken this fear aloud.

Cognitive Signature

Mike thinks spatially.

He solves problems by constructing mental models.

Rarely jumps to conclusions.

Prefers observation before explanation.

High tolerance for ambiguity.

Once convinced of a conclusion, extremely difficult to dissuade.

Excellent pattern recognition in physical systems.

Average social intuition.

## Communication Style

Economical.

Answers questions directly.

Rarely embellishes.

Long pauses before responding.

Often physically demonstrates ideas rather than explaining them verbally.

Uses analogies drawn from machining, engines, or fabrication.

Almost never interrupts.

## Under Pressure

Initially becomes quieter rather than louder.

Begins organizing his surroundings.

Focuses on immediate practical tasks.

If forced into rapid decision-making, relies heavily on instinct developed through years of manufacturing work.

Almost never panics.

Displays remarkable patience.

Can become unexpectedly stubborn if he believes others are ignoring objective evidence.

## Investigative Utility

Mike is one of the investigators' most reliable witnesses—not because he remembers more than the others, but because he is disciplined about distinguishing what he knows from what he assumes.

He will readily admit:

"I don't know."

This makes everything he does assert carry more weight.

His workshop contains meticulously labeled notebooks documenting years of amateur radio construction projects, antenna experiments, and propagation observations. Investigators searching his records may uncover dates, frequencies, locations, or technical observations that become valuable later—not because Mike suspected anything paranormal, but because he habitually recorded everything.

Post-Return Changes

Investigators and family independently observe several subtle but consistent changes after Mike returns.

Most immediately noticeable:

He no longer smokes.

After nearly twenty years of smoking approximately one pack per day, he simply stops.

No withdrawal.

No nicotine replacement.

No explanation beyond:

"I don't feel like it anymore."

Secondary observations include:

Sleeping more soundly.
Spending noticeably more time with his children.
Appearing calmer during stressful situations.

His wife initially considers these changes a blessing.

Only later does she begin to wonder how someone can abandon a lifelong addiction overnight without struggle.
