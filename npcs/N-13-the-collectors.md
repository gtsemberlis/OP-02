# N-13 — The Collectors

## Classification
Unknown organization

## Known Activity
Recovery and preservation of anomalous knowledge and artifacts.

## Threat Assessment
Moderate. Highly disciplined and operationally sophisticated. Intent remains incompletely understood.

## Institutional Overview
The organization designated **the Collectors** enters Program awareness during Operation Airspace when three operatives attempt to recover Dr. Rao's handwritten research. Initial state-espionage assumptions are undermined by their targets, material culture, and lack of electronics.

## Program Analysis
The Collectors appear motivated by preservation rather than power, influence, or profit. Where the Program preserves knowledge so future investigators may learn, the Collectors appear to preserve knowledge so others never have to.

## Observed Philosophy
They distinguish discovering, preserving, and distributing knowledge. They value preservation and treat dissemination with extreme caution. Investigators are obstacles only when positioned between the Collectors and material they intend to recover.

## Operational Behavior
Collector teams demonstrate meticulous planning, restrained force, limited operational footprint, rapid withdrawal, and extensive reconnaissance. During the Alexandria intrusion they ignore valuable technology and proprietary systems while prioritizing handwritten research.

## Material Culture
Nothing disposable. Nothing ornamental. Nothing branded. Clothing, boots, tools, and weapons are repaired and maintained far beyond normal service life. Handwritten notebooks replace electronic storage. No phones, laptops, tablets, GPS devices, smartwatches, or radios are recovered.

## Known Encounter — Operation Airspace
Three Collectors attempt to recover Rao's notebooks. Investigators interrupt them. One operative is killed; two withdraw. Recovered evidence includes notebook fragments, carefully repaired clothing, repeatedly resoled boots, practical firearms, and no electronics.

### Equipment
Soft body armor (Armor 3); 9mm pistol; utility knife; flashlight; lock picks; first-aid kit; paper maps; analog wristwatch; mechanical pencil; handwritten field notebook.

### Operational Doctrine
Observation, recovery, preservation, withdrawal. They never test POW to avoid panic from ordinary combat. They abandon equipment, vehicles, or money before recovered knowledge. They disengage when preservation is no longer possible.

## Known Individuals
**Collector Alpha:** Deceased. Carries H-07. Clothing and boots show extraordinary long-term maintenance.  
**Collector Bravo:** Escaped. Provides security and withdraws without unnecessary fire.  
**Collector Charlie:** Escaped. Primarily responsible for document recovery.

## Operational Knowledge
**Confirmed:** Rao's research exists and concerns a genuine phenomenon.  
**Suspects:** The Program and Alexandria may spread dangerous knowledge.  
**Unknown:** Their full understanding of the phenomenon, internal hierarchy, history, and future objectives.

## Post-Operation Status
The surviving operatives withdraw. The institution remains active regardless of individual losses. Future encounters are likely but not required.

## Operational Notes
Never portray the Collectors as cultists, assassins, fanatics, omniscient agents, or elite action heroes. They are frightening because of competence, patience, maintenance, and institutional continuity.
