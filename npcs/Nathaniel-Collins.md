## Public Record

Nathaniel Collins was born in Utica, New York.

Graduated from Whitesboro High School.

Earned an Associate's Degree in Computer Information Systems from Mohawk Valley Community College before completing a Bachelor's Degree in Information Technology through SUNY Polytechnic Institute.

Worked in private-sector IT before accepting a position with Mohawk Valley Community College.

Currently serves as Senior Network Administrator responsible for:

campus network infrastructure
wireless systems
server administration
cybersecurity
disaster recovery
classroom technology

Employment evaluations consistently describe him as reliable, technically exceptional, and highly professional.

No criminal history.

No civil litigation.

Excellent credit.

No known financial issues.

Maintains an active amateur radio license with particular interest in digital communications and software-defined radio.

## Physical Description

Height: 6'1"

Weight: 182 lbs

Lean, athletic build.

Dark brown hair.

Hazel eyes.

Usually clean-shaven.

Conventionally attractive, with understated good looks that tend to attract more attention than he seems aware of or comfortable with.

Typically dresses in jeans, hiking shoes, and plain button-down shirts.

Often carries a backpack rather than a briefcase.

Frequently wears noise-reducing earbuds while walking across campus, though rarely while interacting with others.

Appears younger than his age.

## Biography

Nathaniel has spent most of his professional life making technology invisible.

When systems function correctly, nobody notices his work.

When they fail, everyone notices immediately.

He prefers that first outcome.

Outside work he maintains several personal interests:

amateur radio
Linux systems
electronics
astronomy
hiking

He avoids social media almost entirely.

His closest friendships are built around shared hobbies rather than frequent social interaction.

Within the amateur radio club he became the unofficial expert on digital modes, packet radio, mesh networking, and software-defined radio.

Older members often joke that Nate can "make radios talk to computers."

## Private Context

Nathaniel has always found structured systems easier to navigate than unstructured social situations.

He is not antisocial.

He simply finds small talk exhausting.

Students who work with him often mistake his quiet demeanor for aloofness.

In reality, he enjoys mentoring anyone genuinely interested in learning.

He simply doesn't initiate conversations.

He has quietly become something of an informal mentor for several engineering and information technology students.

He has never viewed himself as a teacher.

Others increasingly do.

Cognitive Signature

Highly analytical.

Excellent systems thinker.

Naturally models complex interactions mentally.

Prefers evidence over intuition.

Very high tolerance for ambiguity.

Frequently identifies patterns invisible to others.

Occasionally becomes so focused on solving technical problems that he overlooks interpersonal dynamics.

## Communication Style

Concise.

Literal.

Thoughtful.

Answers exactly the question asked.

Avoids exaggeration.

Will happily spend an hour explaining technology if someone demonstrates genuine curiosity.

Dislikes speculation presented as fact.

Frequently qualifies statements with confidence estimates.

## Under Pressure

Initially becomes quieter.

Begins gathering information.

Attempts to isolate variables before acting.

Rarely panics.

Can become cognitively overloaded if forced to make emotionally driven decisions without sufficient information.

Maintains remarkable composure during technical emergencies.

Post-Incident Changes

Nathaniel returns physically unharmed.

Coworkers notice subtle behavioral changes before family or friends do.

Most significantly:

His social anxiety appears markedly reduced.

He attends faculty gatherings voluntarily.

He initiates conversations.

He laughs more often.

Students report that he seems noticeably more approachable.

Nothing feels artificial.

If anything, he appears more comfortable being himself.

Nathaniel recognizes the change immediately.

When asked about it, he says:

"It's strange."

After a pause:

"I still know what anxiety feels like."

Another pause.

"I just... don't experience it anymore."

Unlike Harold, Nathaniel doesn't frame the change philosophically.

Instead, he treats it as an unsolved technical problem.

He has begun quietly documenting every behavioral difference he can identify, building a spreadsheet comparing his memories of himself before and after the disappearance.

Prior Relationship with Victim #5

Nathaniel knew Victim #5 exclusively through Mohawk Valley Community College and the local amateur radio club.

She was enrolled in the Engineering Technology program and occasionally spent time in the campus makerspace, where Nathaniel provided technical support for networking equipment and maintained the club's amateur radio station.

Their interactions were infrequent but positive.

Nathaniel helped her:

configure programming software for a handheld radio,
troubleshoot a faulty antenna connection,
gain access to the campus radio shack,
understand packet radio basics.

Faculty and students universally describe the relationship as professional and appropriate.

Nevertheless, after Victim #5 disappears and Nathaniel disappears shortly thereafter, investigators naturally identify him as a person of interest. Email correspondence, access logs, and witness statements establish that the two knew one another, creating a compelling—but ultimately false—investigative narrative. By the time Nathaniel also vanishes, that theory collapses, leaving investigators to reconsider the significance of their connection from an entirely different perspective.
