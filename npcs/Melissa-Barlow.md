# PER-14 — Melissa Barlow

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

> Status: LOCKED CANONICAL

Melissa Barlow
Basic Information

Age: 54

Occupation: Office Manager

Relationship: Former Wife of David Allen Barlow

Residence: Rome, New York

Divorced from Dave for nine years.

Two adult children together.

Relationship today is cordial but distant.

Public Overview

Melissa is practical, independent, and emotionally mature.

Her marriage to Dave ended not because of a single dramatic event, but through years of financial stress, broken promises, and emotional exhaustion.

Despite the divorce, they maintained a respectful relationship for the sake of their children.

She continued speaking with Dave regularly and remained one of the few people willing to tell him uncomfortable truths.

Personality

Direct.

Patient.

Observant.

Not easily impressed.

Melissa has little interest in assigning blame.

She prefers dealing with reality as it exists rather than imagining how things might have been.

She answers questions honestly, even when the answers make Dave look bad.

Relationship with Dave

Melissa remembers Dave as charismatic, generous, and hardworking.

She also remembers him as someone who constantly believed the next job would solve every financial problem.

Over time, that optimism became denial.

He avoided difficult conversations.

Ignored mounting debt.

Made increasingly poor financial decisions.

Their marriage slowly collapsed under the weight of accumulated stress rather than any lack of affection.

She never stopped caring about him.

She simply stopped believing he would change.

Interview Guide
What Melissa Knows
Dave's HVAC business had struggled financially for several years.
He quietly fell behind on taxes and bookkeeping.
He was deeply embarrassed by these problems and hid the extent of them from almost everyone.
He was never involved with organized crime or violent criminal activity.
He genuinely loved his children despite often disappointing them financially.

Melissa believes Dave's greatest flaw was avoidance, not dishonesty.

What Melissa Doesn't Know

Melissa has no knowledge of:

Delta Green.
Mi-Go.
the Unnatural.
any conspiracy.
any criminal activity beyond Dave's financial misconduct.

She has no explanation for his disappearance.

Behavioral Observations

A successful HUMINT or Psychology roll reveals:

Melissa struggles with a complicated emotion.

She almost smiles while talking about the weeks before Dave disappeared.

Then immediately feels guilty for doing so.

Eventually she says:

"He finally became the man I married."

She quietly adds:

"About twenty years too late."

If Asked About Dave's Financial Problems

Melissa answers openly.

Dave had:

unpaid taxes,
overdue invoices,
bookkeeping errors,
increasing financial pressure.

She believes these issues explain why he seemed anxious around investigators during the final weeks before his disappearance.

She does not believe they explain the disappearance itself.

If Asked About Changes Before His Disappearance

Melissa noticed remarkable differences.

Dave:

admitted mistakes without becoming defensive,
apologized for things he had ignored for years,
began organizing his finances,
called their children more often,
laughed more easily.

For the first time in decades...

...he seemed genuinely at peace.

If Asked About the Revolver

Melissa is surprised.

Dave had never been interested in firearms.

Learning that he purchased one shortly before disappearing seems completely out of character.

After thinking for a moment, she says:

"He wasn't buying a gun."

She pauses.

"He was buying the feeling that maybe everything wasn't falling apart."

If Asked About Dave's Character

Melissa refuses to let investigators reduce Dave to either a victim or a criminal.

She says:

"Dave made mistakes."

"Some of them were expensive."

Another pause.

"But making mistakes isn't the same thing as being a bad man."

Investigative Value

Melissa provides investigators with:

Context for Dave's financial difficulties.
A credible explanation for his apparent nervousness during the FBI investigation.
Independent confirmation that his personality changed dramatically before disappearing.
Strong evidence that his criminal conduct was limited to financial misconduct rather than violence.

Her testimony also helps separate Dave's understandable fear of legal consequences from the impossible circumstances surrounding the disappearances.

## Handler Notes

Melissa serves an important narrative purpose by preventing Dave from becoming either a stereotype or a red herring.

She neither idealizes nor condemns him. Instead, she presents a nuanced portrait of a fundamentally decent man whose flaws cost him his marriage but never extinguished his humanity.

Her interview also delivers one of the operation's most poignant realizations: Dave didn't become a different person before he disappeared—he became the person he had always wanted to be. That transformation is both beautiful and deeply unsettling because it raises the central question of Episode 2:

If something can remove the parts of us that hurt ourselves and others... is the person left behind still us?
