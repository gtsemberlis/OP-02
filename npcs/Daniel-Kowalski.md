# PER-08 — Daniel Kowalski

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Public Overview

Daniel Kowalski is a conscientious, level-headed park ranger who knows Hogsback State Forest better than almost anyone.

Visitors recognize him as approachable and knowledgeable. Local hikers know him by name.

He enjoys the solitude of backcountry patrols and takes genuine pride in maintaining the trails and keeping visitors safe.

If something appears unusual in the forest, his instinct is never to panic—it is to investigate, document, and resolve it.

Personality

Patient.

Observant.

Professional.

Self-reliant.

Kowalski dislikes unnecessary drama and rarely jumps to conclusions. He believes nearly every problem has a practical explanation if someone is willing to look carefully enough.

He has a dry sense of humor and is well liked by both local law enforcement and county search-and-rescue volunteers.

## Background

Raised in Rome, New York.

Served six years in the New York Army National Guard before joining the state park system.

Worked seasonal park maintenance while attending college before becoming a full-time ranger.

Known for volunteering for remote patrol assignments and assisting in difficult search-and-rescue operations.

Outside of work he enjoys fishing, camping, and restoring old canoes.

Interview Guide (Before the Timeline Advances)

If investigators encounter Ranger Kowalski before his disappearance, they find an experienced public servant who is happy to answer questions.

He mentions one persistent annoyance.

"I've been getting some odd interference on the handheld out in the north end of the forest."

He assumes it is one of four possibilities:

a damaged repeater,
an unauthorized transmitter,
atmospheric interference,
failing radio equipment.

If asked whether it concerns him, he shrugs.

"Not really."

"Just means I have to get closer before dispatch can hear me."

If asked to demonstrate, he keys his radio.

Beneath otherwise normal transmissions, investigators can hear a faint, rhythmic buzzing that occasionally overwhelms the speaker before fading again.

Kowalski finds it mildly irritating.

Nothing more.

Timeline Event

At the Handler's discretion, Daniel Kowalski becomes the operation's sixth disappearance.

While conducting a routine patrol in Hogsback State Forest, he contacts dispatch.

"Dispatch, Ranger Three."

"Go ahead."

"Getting that interference again. Stronger than usual."

A brief pause.

"I'm going to check it out."

Dispatch acknowledges.

This is his final transmission.

Approximately thirty to sixty minutes later:

Kowalski misses a scheduled check-in.
His patrol truck is located along a forest access road.
His handheld radio remains inside the vehicle.
The radio is still powered on.
The buzzing is significantly stronger than on previous recordings.

There are no signs of a struggle.

No obvious trail.

No indication of where he went.

Investigative Value

Kowalski's disappearance marks the transition from historical investigation to active containment.

It demonstrates that:

the phenomenon remains active,
victims are not selected because of the amateur radio club,
Hogsback State Forest is the true geographic commonality,
ordinary people continue to be at risk.

His abandoned radio becomes one of the operation's most important physical artifacts, providing investigators with the strongest recording yet of the anomalous buzzing.

## Handler Notes

Daniel Kowalski should be portrayed as an ordinary professional doing an ordinary job exceptionally well.

His disappearance should never feel like the consequence of recklessness or horror-movie curiosity. Every decision he makes is exactly what a competent park ranger would do: investigate unexplained radio interference in an isolated section of the forest.

That ordinariness is what gives the scene its emotional impact. Unlike the first five victims, Kowalski disappears while the investigators are present. He is the first person they might have been able to save—and the moment he vanishes, the investigation ceases to be about understanding the past and becomes about preventing the next disappearance. I think that makes him a worthy "full" character despite his relatively brief appearance.
