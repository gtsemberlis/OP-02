# PER-09 — Evan Brooks

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Evan Brooks
Basic Information

Age: 46

Occupation: Software Engineer (Remote)

Secondary Occupation: Administrator, SaucerWatch.org

Residence: Albany, New York

Married.

No children.

Public Overview

Evan Brooks serves as one of the volunteer administrators for SaucerWatch, a long-running website dedicated to collecting reports of unidentified aerial phenomena and other unexplained events throughout the northeastern United States.

His responsibilities include reviewing new submissions, organizing the site's growing archive, corresponding with witnesses, and coordinating volunteer field investigators.

Unlike many conspiracy enthusiasts, Brooks actively discourages fabricated stories and encourages contributors to submit original evidence whenever possible.

Over the years he has accumulated thousands of photographs, recordings, witness statements, radar screenshots, and radio logs.

Most of it is worthless.

Some of it is genuinely strange.

Brooks cannot reliably distinguish between the two.

Personality

Intelligent.

Methodical.

Curious.

Friendly.

Hopelessly pattern-seeking.

Brooks genuinely wants to discover the truth.

He is not trying to deceive anyone.

His greatest weakness is believing every unexplained event belongs to the same underlying mystery.

## Background

Computer Science degree.

Works remotely for a software company.

Has volunteered with SaucerWatch for well over a decade and now serves as one of its senior administrators.

Within the community he is respected for his careful documentation and willingness to spend countless hours organizing reports that few other volunteers would bother to catalog.

He has never sought publicity and has little interest in becoming a public figure.

His wife considers SaucerWatch an eccentric but harmless hobby.

Interview Guide

Brooks is enthusiastic about meeting investigators he believes are taking anomalous reports seriously.

He immediately asks far more questions than he answers.

Examples:

"What agency are you with?"

"Have you seen something like this before?"

"Is this connected to any other cases?"

He assumes the investigators represent some federal office with an interest in unexplained aerial phenomena.

He has no knowledge of Delta Green or any other covert organization.

What Brooks Knows

He received Nate Collins' SaucerWatch submission.

It included:

radio recordings,
SDR waterfall captures,
GPS track,
timeline,
written narrative.

Brooks immediately recognized it as unusual—not because he understood it, but because of the quantity and quality of supporting documentation.

He has already begun comparing the submission against older reports archived by SaucerWatch.

He has identified several superficially similar cases from:

the Adirondacks,
northern Pennsylvania,
western Massachusetts.

Whether those reports are actually related is impossible to determine.

What Brooks Doesn't Know

He has no understanding of the Mi-Go.

No knowledge of Delta Green.

No awareness that the historical reports in the SaucerWatch archive likely represent multiple unrelated Unnatural phenomena.

His organizational system is fundamentally flawed.

Everything unexplained is cataloged together.

Investigative Value

Brooks represents an important thematic contrast.

The Program preserves information through disciplined tradecraft.

SaucerWatch preserves information through enthusiastic civilian investigation.

Ironically, both organizations collect genuine anomalous evidence.

Only one possesses a rigorous methodology for evaluating it.

## Handler Notes

Brooks should never be portrayed as gullible or ridiculous.

He is intelligent, sincere, and exceptionally diligent.

His weakness is methodological rather than intellectual. He assumes that every unexplained event belongs to a single category of phenomenon and therefore searches for universal explanations where none exist.

Investigators who dismiss him entirely may lose access to useful evidence.

Investigators who rely on him uncritically risk following compelling but incorrect patterns.
