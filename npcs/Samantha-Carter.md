# PER-04 — Samantha Carter

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Known As: "Sam"

Residence: Lives with parents in Whitesboro, New York

Marital Status: Single

Education:

Engineering Technology Program

Part-time employee at Harbor Freight Tools in New Hartford.

## Public Record

Samantha Carter was born in Utica, New York.

Graduated from Whitesboro High School.

Consistently above-average academic performance, particularly in mathematics, physics, and technical education courses.

No disciplinary history.

No criminal record.

No financial concerns.

Limited social media presence consisting primarily of photographs of electronics projects, amateur radio equipment, and hiking trips.

Faculty describe her as exceptionally capable but quiet.

No known romantic relationships.

Employment evaluations consistently describe her as dependable, punctual, and technically curious.

## Physical Description

Height: 5'7"

Weight: 138 lbs

Athletic build.

Shoulder-length dark blonde hair, usually tied back in a ponytail.

Gray-green eyes.

Minimal makeup.

Usually dressed in jeans, hiking boots, hooded sweatshirts, and work shirts from Harbor Freight.

Frequently carries a worn backpack containing notebooks, tools, and electronics components.

Often has minor cuts or scratches on her hands from mechanical projects.

Generally maintains little awareness of fashion or appearance beyond practicality.

## Biography

Sam grew up taking things apart.

Her parents learned early that broken household electronics often returned in better condition than before.

Rather than discourage her curiosity, they encouraged it.

She became active in robotics competitions during high school before discovering amateur radio through a physics teacher.

Unlike many club members, she entered the hobby from computers rather than traditional radio.

She quickly became fascinated with digital communications, propagation, and antenna design.

Older members immediately recognized both her enthusiasm and willingness to learn.

The club gradually adopted her as something of a collective granddaughter.

## Private Context

Sam has never been particularly concerned with fitting in.

She often misses sarcasm.

She dislikes crowded social gatherings.

Eye contact feels uncomfortable.

Unexpected changes in plans frustrate her more than most people.

She prefers direct communication.

People who know her well recognize that beneath her quiet exterior is an exceptionally dry sense of humor that appears only when she feels completely comfortable.

She deeply admires several older members of the amateur radio club, particularly Harold Whitaker and Nate Collins, seeing them less as authority figures than as people who genuinely enjoy answering difficult questions.

Cognitive Signature

Exceptionally curious.

Rapid learner.

Excellent spatial reasoning.

Strong systems thinking.

Comfortable with abstraction.

Notices inconsistencies others overlook.

Occasionally becomes intensely focused on technical problems, losing awareness of time.

Very honest.

Rarely considers how blunt observations may affect others emotionally.

## Communication Style

Literal.

Brief.

Direct.

Frequently answers questions with exactly the information requested.

Rarely volunteers additional context unless prompted.

Comfortable discussing technical subjects for hours.

Sometimes pauses for several seconds before responding while mentally organizing her thoughts.

## Under Pressure

Initially withdraws.

Speaks less.

Attempts to solve problems independently.

Strong preference for routine causes unexpected situations to feel overwhelming.

Once presented with a clearly defined objective, however, she becomes remarkably composed and focused.

She responds better to calm instruction than emotional reassurance.

## Investigative Utility

Sam keeps surprisingly thorough engineering notebooks despite her age.

Investigators may recover:

class notes
electronics sketches
antenna experiments
propagation observations
digital mode logs
personal engineering projects

Unlike Harold's formal engineering journals or Nate's digital documentation, Sam's notebooks are exploratory.

Margins are filled with ideas, questions, diagrams, and half-finished experiments.

They provide insight into how she thinks more than what she knows.

Investigators may also find email correspondence with instructors, Nate Collins, and amateur radio club members documenting entirely ordinary mentorship and technical discussions.

Prior Relationship with Nate Collins

Sam first met Nate through Mohawk Valley Community College after expressing interest in the campus amateur radio station.

Nate assisted her with:

programming her first handheld radio,
configuring packet radio software,
troubleshooting antenna issues,
gaining access to the campus radio shack.

The relationship remained entirely professional and centered on shared technical interests.

Faculty members describe Nate as one of several informal mentors who encouraged promising students.

Sam consistently spoke of Nate with respect, viewing him as someone whose knowledge she hoped to one day match.

Following Sam's disappearance, this relationship becomes one of the FBI's earliest investigative leads.

When Nate disappears shortly afterward, the theory shifts dramatically.

Post-Incident Changes

Sam returns physically healthy.

Medical examinations reveal no abnormalities.

Her parents immediately notice something they have quietly hoped for since childhood.

She makes eye contact.

Not constantly.

Not unnaturally.

Simply...

Comfortably.

Family dinners become easier.

Conversations flow more naturally.

She initiates them.

Her mother tearfully tells investigators:

"It's like she's finally comfortable."

Her father agrees.

Then hesitates.

"...and I don't know why that scares me."

Sam herself struggles to articulate the difference.

When asked directly, she thinks for a long moment before answering:

"It used to feel like people were... loud."

Another pause.

"Now they're not."

She doesn't describe herself as happier.

She doesn't feel transformed.

She simply experiences social interaction as less mentally taxing than before.

She regards this as a welcome change, but one she cannot explain.

Design Note (LOCKED)

Sam is not defined by a diagnosis. The operation never labels her as autistic, nor should NPCs speak about her in clinical terms. Instead, she is portrayed as a young woman with a literal communication style, focused interests, and a preference for structure—traits that are recognizable without becoming her identity.

Her post-incident change is intentionally subtle. The horror is not that she becomes "normal"; it is that a deeply ingrained aspect of how she experiences the world has been altered without her knowledge or consent. Like the other victims, the question is not whether the change is beneficial, but whether anyone has the right to decide what parts of a person should be "improved." I think that makes her a fitting final victim and a powerful emotional anchor for the investigation.
