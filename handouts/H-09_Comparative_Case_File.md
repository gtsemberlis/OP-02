To:

Assigned Case Officers

Subject:

Use of Comparative Files

Comparable cases exist to provide operational context.

They are not predictive.

Avoid forcing current investigations to conform to historical precedent.

Every comparable file exists because previous investigators believed the current situation had no precedent.

Read for mistakes.

Not answers.

I think that last sentence may become one of my favorite lines in the campaign.

Read for mistakes. Not answers.

That is the Program.

Perfectly distilled.

Notice how different that is from the Archive.

The Archive says:

Preserve abandoned interpretations.

The Program says:

Study previous mistakes.

One preserves intellectual history.

The other preserves operational experience.

Same destination.

Different purpose.

Then...
