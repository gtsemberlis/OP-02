# LOC-05 — Samantha Carter Residence

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Samantha Carter Residence
## Where is it?

A modest single-family home in a quiet residential neighborhood in Utica, approximately fifteen minutes from Mohawk Valley Community College.

The house sits on a tree-lined street of similar homes. Flower beds border the front walkway. A small American flag hangs from the porch in late June, part of the neighborhood's preparations for Independence Day. Neighbors quietly watch unfamiliar vehicles from their windows. Everyone knows what happened to Samantha.

## What are the sights, sounds, and atmosphere?

The Carter residence feels warm, comfortable, and unmistakably lived in.

The air carries the smell of fresh coffee and laundry detergent. Family photographs line the hallway, documenting vacations, birthdays, and Samantha's high school graduation. A local news station murmurs from the living room television, its volume turned down almost to the point of being background noise.

Conversation inside the house feels careful.

Everyone is trying to avoid saying the wrong thing.

The family has settled into an uneasy routine after Samantha's return, but there is a constant sense that normal life has become something they are performing rather than experiencing.

Silences linger just a little too long.

Samantha's Room

Samantha's bedroom immediately communicates who she is.

The room is tidy without being immaculate. It's the room of someone balancing school, work, and ordinary life.

A neatly made twin bed sits beneath a window overlooking the backyard.

The walls display framed photographs rather than posters—friends from high school, family vacations in the Adirondacks, and candid pictures from college events. A corkboard above her desk holds class schedules, grocery lists, concert ticket stubs, and snapshots with classmates.

Her desk is organized but obviously used.

Open textbooks sit beside highlighted notebooks covering biology, psychology, and general education courses. A laptop remains plugged into its charger, surrounded by colorful pens, sticky notes, and an oversized coffee mug filled with pencils.

A bookshelf contains a mixture of required reading, mystery novels, hiking guides she recently checked out from the library, and several well-worn paperbacks. None suggest an obsession with UFOs or the paranormal.

A hoodie embroidered with the Mohawk Valley Community College logo hangs over the back of her chair.

A pair of well-used running shoes rests beside the closet.

On the dresser are small reminders of an ordinary life:

a bottle of inexpensive perfume,
a bracelet from a friend,
movie ticket stubs tucked into a jewelry tray,
a graduation tassel,
a framed photograph of Samantha laughing with her parents.

Nothing in the room suggests tragedy.

It feels like someone left for class and simply never finished unpacking the day.

That normalcy becomes one of the room's defining characteristics.

Who is associated with it?
Samantha Carter

Primary interview subject.

Samantha is cooperative, thoughtful, and genuinely wants to help.

She is frustrated by her inability to remember what happened.

She never attempts to deceive the investigators.

Thomas Carter

Father.

Protective and increasingly skeptical of repeated questioning.

His instinct is to shield Samantha from additional stress.

Linda Carter

Mother.

Exhausted but hopeful.

She quietly believes something happened that no one has been able to explain.

What can be learned here?

This location provides emotional context more than forensic evidence.

Investigators may learn:

Samantha has resumed most of her daily routine.
She sleeps poorly despite insisting she feels physically well.
She has become unusually interested in Hogsback State Forest despite having rarely visited before her disappearance.
She experiences recurring dreams that disappear moments after waking.
She occasionally pauses in conversation as though trying to remember something just beyond reach.
She sincerely believes the missing time contained something important.

If questioned gently, Samantha can describe sensations rather than memories:

a persistent electrical buzzing,
brilliant white light,
overwhelming silence,
the certainty that someone was observing her without malice or anger.

These details are individually ambiguous but become significant when compared with the accounts of the other victims.

What artifacts can be found here?

Investigators may discover:

Samantha's recovered clothing from the day she disappeared.
A personal journal filled with increasingly frustrated attempts to remember the missing days.
Her current class schedule from Mohawk Valley Community College.
Family photographs taken before and after the disappearance.
A collection of handwritten notes attempting to reconstruct the timeline of her disappearance.

If the investigators return after their initial interview, additional materials may appear:

printed satellite imagery of Hogsback State Forest,
trail maps with sections highlighted,
handwritten questions about specific locations,
a backpack partially packed for another trip into the woods.
How does this location change after the investigators visit?

The investigators' interview validates Samantha's belief that her experience was real.

Instead of encouraging closure, it convinces her that answers still exist.

She quietly begins preparing to return to Hogsback State Forest.

Her parents remain unaware of her plans.

The atmosphere within the house becomes increasingly strained as Samantha withdraws emotionally while appearing outwardly calm.

What do investigators find on a return visit?

The house feels different.

Samantha is distracted.

Her room is no longer simply a student's bedroom.

Books about local history have appeared beside her textbooks. Trail maps lie open across the desk. Search results about disappearances, radio interference, and unexplained aerial phenomena remain open on her laptop.

If she has not yet left, investigators may realize she has already decided to return to the forest—they have merely arrived before she acts.

If they arrive after her departure:

Her bed has been made.

Her backpack is gone.

Her hiking boots are missing from beside the closet.

The desk chair has been pushed neatly under the desk.

A handwritten note rests beside her laptop.

Mom, Dad—I'm sorry. I think I know where I need to go. I'll be home soon.

The note contains no destination.

Her parents believe she has gone for a drive to clear her head.

The investigators immediately recognize that she has returned to Hogsback State Forest.

Design Notes

The emotional purpose of Samantha's residence is not to reveal the mystery.

It is to establish what is at stake.

Every detail should reinforce that Samantha is an ordinary young woman with an ordinary life—family photographs, unfinished coursework, ticket stubs, borrowed library books, and plans for the future. If the operation ultimately reaches the Mi-Go operating chamber and the investigators find a biological cylinder labeled SAMANTHA CARTER, the horror comes not from the cylinder itself, but from the realization that they have already stood in her room, seen the life she expected to continue, and now understand how completely that ordinary world has collided with something profoundly alien.
