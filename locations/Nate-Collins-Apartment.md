# LOC-06 — Nate Collins Apartment

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Nate Collins Apartment
## Where is it?

Nate rents a one-bedroom apartment in a recently renovated brick building near downtown Utica, within easy driving distance of Mohawk Valley Community College and approximately thirty minutes from Hogsback State Forest.

The building is typical of young professionals and graduate students. Secure entry, assigned parking, bicycles chained outside, and package deliveries stacked in the lobby.

His apartment occupies the second floor overlooking a side street lined with coffee shops and small businesses.

Nothing about the exterior suggests anything unusual.

## What are the sights, sounds, and atmosphere?

The apartment immediately feels different from the previous locations.

It belongs to someone who enjoys technology.

Computer fans hum quietly.

Several monitors glow across the room.

An SDR waterfall display slowly scrolls across one screen.

A soldering station sits neatly organized beside small electronics projects.

Portable radios, antennas, cables, USB adapters, batteries, and GPS receivers are arranged with surprising care.

Unlike David's meticulous order, Nate's organization reflects active hobbies.

Every object appears to have been used recently.

Music may be playing quietly through desktop speakers.

Coffee mugs occupy nearly every flat surface.

The apartment smells faintly of coffee, warm electronics, and solder.

Despite the equipment, the apartment doesn't feel obsessive.

It feels enthusiastic.

The investigators quickly understand that Nate enjoys figuring out how things work.

Nate's Office

Nearly half the apartment has been converted into a workspace.

Three computer monitors dominate the desk.

One displays SDR software.

Another contains GIS mapping software.

The third alternates between photography software and browser tabs documenting radio propagation, GPS anomalies, and aviation.

Mounted on the wall are several framed landscape photographs, many taken in Hogsback State Forest.

Shelves contain:

handheld radios

amateur photography equipment
drones
camera lenses
GPS units

field notebooks

A whiteboard lists future hiking trips, radio frequencies to monitor, and equipment upgrades.

Unlike Hal's notebooks, Nate's notes are informal.

Ideas.

Experiments.

Questions.

He enjoys collecting data simply because it's interesting.

One corner of the room contains hiking gear packed into plastic storage bins.

A camera backpack sits partially unpacked beside the desk.

Who is associated with it?
Nate Collins

Primary interview.

Friendly.

Curious.

Talkative.

Nate enjoys explaining his equipment.

He is fascinated by unexplained data but is careful not to jump to conclusions.

Unlike some paranormal enthusiasts, Nate understands the importance of documenting observations accurately.

The investigators quickly realize he is far more credible than they expected.

What can be learned there?

This location provides the operation's strongest forensic evidence.

Investigators may learn:

Nate routinely records wide-band radio spectrum during outdoor trips.
He synchronizes radio recordings with GPS tracks and camera timestamps.
His disappearance interrupted an otherwise ordinary hiking excursion.
Multiple independent devices recorded anomalies at approximately the same time.
The missing time corresponds closely with unexplained gaps in several digital datasets.
Nate has become convinced that whatever happened produced measurable physical effects.

Unlike every previous witness, Nate possesses evidence that exists independently of memory.

What artifacts can be found there?

Investigators may discover:

Original SDR recordings containing the anomalous buzzing.
Waterfall display captures showing unexplained broadband interference.
GPS track logs with impossible positional data.
High-resolution photographs taken immediately before the disappearance.
Camera metadata establishing an accurate timeline.
Drone footage of Hogsback State Forest.
Field notebooks documenting weather, equipment settings, and observations.
Backup copies stored on external hard drives.

Taken together, these represent the operation's most complete collection of civilian evidence.

How does this location change after the investigators visit?

The investigators validate Nate's suspicion that his experience deserves serious attention.

Unlike David, he becomes more confident.

Unlike Samantha, he doesn't return to the forest immediately.

Instead, he shares his findings.

Within hours, Nate uploads his complete documentation to SaucerWatch.

His upload includes:

SDR recordings
photographs
GPS logs
written timeline
equipment specifications
observations

The upload quickly attracts attention from Evan Brooks and the SaucerWatch community.

The apartment effectively becomes the origin point of a growing civilian investigation.

What do investigators find on a return visit?

The apartment has become noticeably busier.

Additional browser tabs fill Nate's monitors.

His inbox contains dozens of messages from SaucerWatch members.

Video calls with Evan Brooks may already be underway.

Printed maps cover the desk.

Comment threads compare Nate's recordings with decades of other unexplained reports.

Nate is excited.

Not because he believes he has solved the mystery.

Because, for the first time, he feels that other people are taking his evidence seriously.

He eagerly shares everything.

He has nothing to hide.

Unfortunately, he has unintentionally made the investigation far more difficult.

By the investigators' second visit, Brooks is already planning his trip to Oneida County.

Design Notes

Nate's apartment represents the democratization of evidence.

Unlike Hal Whitaker's carefully maintained amateur radio station or the FBI's professional investigation, Nate's documentation comes from consumer technology available to almost anyone: software-defined radios, GPS receivers, digital cameras, drones, and laptops. His evidence demonstrates that the modern world is saturated with sensors capable of recording phenomena their owners do not fully understand.

The location also serves as the narrative hinge between the private investigation and the public one. Until the investigators meet Nate, the operation remains largely contained within local law enforcement and the Program. After they leave, Nate's entirely reasonable decision to share well-documented observations transforms an isolated case into a growing civilian curiosity. The apartment is therefore not just where evidence is stored—it is where the investigation escapes the control of every official agency involved.
