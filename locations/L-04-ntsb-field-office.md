# L-04 — NTSB Field Office

## Owner / Responsible Organization
National Transportation Safety Board

## Accessibility
Restricted federal investigation workspace. Access is granted to assigned personnel, authorized liaisons, and scheduled witnesses.

## Operational Status
Temporary operational center supporting the O'Hare accident investigation.

## Overview
The NTSB Field Office represents disciplined professional skepticism. Witness accounts, records, and physical evidence are evaluated using established methods before extraordinary conclusions are considered.

## Operational Function
L-04 supports witness coordination, interview preparation, testimony comparison, and separation of direct observation from later interpretation.

## Physical Description
A practical temporary investigation headquarters containing interview rooms, evidence workspaces, briefing areas, records stations, and administrative offices. Furnishings are functional and impersonal.

## Environmental Characteristics
The atmosphere is quiet, procedural, and respectful. Interviews are scheduled, documented, and conducted without leading questions. Personnel focus on chronology, corroboration, human factors, and uncertainty rather than speculation.

Plain white office paper and crayons are available for Owen while adults speak nearby. Nothing about the materials suggests evidentiary intent.

## Editorial Theme
**Method**

Every conventional explanation deserves honest examination before rejection. Professional skepticism narrows the field without forcing certainty.

## GM Guidance
Reward attentive listening rather than clever interrogation. No witness possesses the answer. The location functions best when investigators compare several incomplete but sincere accounts.

Karen Delgado should remain a facilitator of good process, not an obstacle or secret source of unnatural knowledge.

## Operational Notes
The office should feel emotionally safer than the accident site. That safety allows witnesses—especially Sarah and Owen—to behave naturally.
