# L-03 — Dr. Rao's Office

## Owner / Responsible Organization
Alexandria Aviation Systems; assigned to Dr. Ananya Rao.

## Accessibility
Controlled corporate workspace. Investigators receive access through Alexandria authorization or Dr. Rao's cooperation.

## Operational Status
Active research office. Later subject to focused interest from the Collectors through their pursuit of Rao's handwritten work.

## Overview
Dr. Rao's office is the most personal space within Alexandria. It reveals the human cost of sustained intellectual honesty and allows investigators to understand the researcher before judging her conclusions.

## Operational Function
L-03 supports the first substantive conversation with Rao, examination of A-05, and recognition that her work has moved beyond routine corporate analysis without abandoning scientific discipline.

## Physical Description
A compact office crowded with whiteboards, printed charts, handwritten calculations, annotated aviation reports, organized stacks of paper, and notebooks. The room is heavily used but not neglected. Every surface reflects active comparison, revision, and documentation.

## Environmental Characteristics
The office is quiet enough for careful conversation but visually dense with months of work. Digital analysis remains present, but handwritten notes dominate the most speculative and personally significant material.

A locked cabinet or protected storage area contains private notebooks Rao is not yet ready to circulate. Her caution reflects methodology, not concealment.

## Areas of Interest
- Whiteboards showing probability trees and cross-modal comparisons
- Printed historical incident data
- Rao's handwritten notebooks
- Locked storage for unfinished work
- Personal indicators of exhaustion and sustained focus

## Editorial Theme
**Empathy**

Understanding the person becomes as important as understanding the research. Knowledge has a human cost, and investigators should understand Rao's discipline before judging where it has led her.

## GM Guidance
Avoid presenting the office as the workspace of an unstable genius. It belongs to a scientist whose commitment to evidence has reshaped her daily life.

Let investigators discover strain through ordinary details—missed meals, dense annotations, accumulated drafts—without implying incoherence or prophecy.

## Operational Notes
L-03 remains distinct from any private residence. Personal vulnerability is expressed through work habits and research material rather than domestic intrusion.
