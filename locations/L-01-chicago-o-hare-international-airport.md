# L-01 — Chicago O'Hare International Airport

## Owner / Responsible Organization
Chicago Department of Aviation, with FAA and NTSB authority relevant to the operation.

## Accessibility
Restricted according to operational area. Public terminals remain accessible; accident, interview, and runway-observation areas require official credentials and escort.

## Operational Status
Active international airport operating under temporary restrictions following the hard landing.

## Overview
O'Hare is the initiating environment of Operation Airspace and the investigators' first contact with evidence that resists conventional explanation. It remains a functioning airport throughout the investigation, grounding every extraordinary observation in familiar aviation procedure.

## Operational Function
L-01 supports the initiating accident investigation, civilian witness access, and the controlled runway observation. It presents evidence before anyone understands what the evidence means.

## Physical Description
A vast, active international airport of terminals, service roads, taxiways, runways, operations spaces, hangars, and secured airside infrastructure. Emergency activity around the damaged aircraft gradually gives way to disciplined evidence recovery and restricted normal operations.

## Environmental Characteristics
The airport is defined by controlled motion: arriving aircraft, ground vehicles, radio traffic, maintenance teams, investigators, and airport personnel following established procedure. The ordinary system continues functioning around the anomaly.

During the runway observation, the same environment becomes unnaturally quiet without becoming theatrical. Personnel remain focused on instrumentation, traffic control, and safety. The phenomenon is extraordinary because it passes through an otherwise ordinary operational landscape.

## Areas of Interest
- Accident aircraft and secured investigation perimeter
- Air traffic control and recorded communications
- Passenger and crew processing areas
- NTSB interview coordination spaces
- Restricted runway environment used for the controlled observation
- Alexandria sensor equipment and observation positions

## Editorial Theme
**Observation and Verification**

At the beginning of the operation, reality presents evidence before explanation. At the runway observation, the investigators return to the same environment and test whether reality behaves consistently.

## GM Guidance
Present O'Hare as an environment of competent professionals doing familiar work. The location becomes unsettling because credible systems and credible people independently agree on an impossible observation.

The runway sequence should remain quiet and restrained. The emotional impact comes from confirmation, not spectacle.

## Operational Notes
O'Hare is both public infrastructure and a collection of tightly controlled operational zones. Investigator access should feel legitimate but bounded by professional procedure.
