# Christopher Donnelly Residence

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

Christopher Donnelly Residence
Where is it?

Christopher lives alone in a modest townhouse on the outskirts of Utica, approximately twenty-five minutes from Hogsback State Forest.

The neighborhood consists of newer developments occupied by tradespeople, technicians, and young families. The homes are practical, well maintained, and largely indistinguishable from one another.

His garage door is often open during daylight hours, revealing a neatly organized workshop rather than a place to park a vehicle.

What are the sights, sounds, and atmosphere?

Christopher's home immediately feels purposeful.

The television is off.

Music isn't playing.

Instead, the house is remarkably quiet.

The only sounds are the hum of a desktop computer, the ticking of a wall clock, and occasionally the muted chatter of a police scanner or amateur radio monitoring local traffic.

The home is clean but utilitarian.

Furniture is comfortable without being decorative.

Everything appears selected because it performs a function.

Unlike David's house, which feels increasingly defensive, Christopher's home feels active.

Someone is working.

Someone is trying to solve a problem.

Christopher's Workshop / Office

The converted garage serves as Christopher's workshop.

A sturdy workbench occupies one wall beneath shelves lined with labeled storage bins.

Electronic components.

Connectors.

Coaxial cable.

Network switches.

Fiber optic tools.

Multimeters.

Oscilloscopes.

Soldering equipment.

Everything is neatly arranged and obviously used.

A desktop computer sits on a side bench connected to multiple monitors displaying maps, utility infrastructure diagrams, and maintenance documentation.

Whiteboards cover one wall.

Most contain ordinary work notes.

One section has been erased and rewritten repeatedly.

Dates.

Times.

Locations.

Questions.

Pinned beside the whiteboard are printouts of utility maps, cellular tower locations, and transmission infrastructure surrounding Hogsback State Forest.

Unlike Nate, Christopher isn't fascinated by anomalies.

He's trying to determine what physical system could possibly produce them.

Who is associated with it?
Christopher Donnelly

Primary interview.

Thoughtful.

Analytical.

Reserved.

Christopher approaches every conversation like a troubleshooting exercise.

He rarely speculates.

Instead, he asks increasingly precise questions.

He does not believe the investigators are hiding anything.

He believes they simply haven't identified the correct failure yet.

After speaking with them, he quietly begins his own investigation.

What can be learned there?

Christopher contributes a systems perspective unavailable anywhere else.

Investigators may learn:

He remembers almost nothing of the disappearance itself.
His professional background makes him skeptical of coincidence.
He believes the disappearances must correspond to a physical process rather than random events.
He has begun comparing utility outages, communications interruptions, weather records, and maintenance logs.
None of the conventional explanations fit.

Christopher repeatedly returns to the same conclusion:

Something affected multiple independent systems simultaneously.

He simply cannot explain what.

What artifacts can be found there?

Utility maintenance maps.

Topographic maps annotated with suspected signal paths.
Hand-drawn diagrams attempting to correlate each victim's disappearance with infrastructure locations.

Unlike Hal's evidence, Christopher's work consists almost entirely of analysis rather than observation.

How does this location change after the investigators visit?

The investigators unintentionally confirm Christopher's suspicion that the disappearances deserve continued investigation.

Rather than waiting for answers, he begins gathering additional information himself.

He contacts former coworkers.

Requests maintenance records.

Reviews archived communications logs.

Visits portions of the utility network surrounding Hogsback State Forest.

His investigation is intelligent.

It is also increasingly dangerous.

Without realizing it, Christopher begins moving toward the same locations the investigators themselves are attempting to understand.

What do investigators find on a return visit?

The workshop has changed.

The whiteboards are now nearly full.

Colored string connects maps across one wall.

Fresh printouts cover the workbench.

Several computer monitors display aerial imagery and infrastructure overlays simultaneously.

Christopher has stopped looking for isolated failures.

He's searching for patterns.

He enthusiastically shares new discoveries.

Most prove to be dead ends.

Some, however, independently reproduce observations the investigators have already made.

If questioned carefully, Christopher admits something unexpected.

"I don't think I'm looking at equipment failures anymore."

He hesitates before continuing.

"I think... whatever happened... interacted with the equipment."

It is the first time he openly acknowledges that the cause may lie outside conventional engineering.

The realization clearly troubles him.

Design Notes

Christopher's residence represents systems thinking.

Where Hal Whitaker documents observations, Nate Collins captures measurements, Samantha Carter embodies lived experience, and David Barlow struggles to preserve an ordinary explanation, Christopher asks a fundamentally different question:

What kind of system could produce these effects?

His workshop becomes the investigation's engineering mind. Every map, schematic, maintenance record, and network diagram reflects an effort to understand the disappearances as interactions between physical infrastructure and an unknown external influence. He never leaps to extraordinary conclusions; instead, he systematically eliminates ordinary ones.

This also makes Christopher's post-interview arc especially compelling. He does not descend into obsession or paranoia. He behaves exactly as a competent engineer would: he gathers more data. That quiet professionalism gradually leads him toward the same truth the investigators are uncovering, making him one of the operation's most capable—and most vulnerable—civilian participants.
