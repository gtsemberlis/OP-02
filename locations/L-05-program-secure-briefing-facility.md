# L-05 — Program Secure Briefing Facility

## Owner / Responsible Organization
The Program

## Accessibility
Restricted to cleared Program personnel and specifically authorized investigators.

## Operational Status
Active secure briefing, archival, and debriefing environment.

## Overview
The Program Secure Briefing Facility frames Operation Airspace at the institutional level. It is where investigators receive the assignment, compare their conclusions against preserved precedent, and convert incomplete understanding into an operational recommendation.

## Operational Function
L-05 supports the initial briefing, Handler contact, final debrief, and access to A-09 — Comparative Case File.

## Physical Description
An intentionally unremarkable federal workspace: controlled entry, plain conference room, secure communications, locked storage, and archival material kept out of public systems. Nothing advertises the Program's true function.

## Environmental Characteristics
The facility is quiet, controlled, and deliberately free of theatrical secrecy. Files, equipment, and furnishings are maintained for use rather than display. The environment reflects continuity and institutional memory.

## Editorial Theme
**Stewardship**

Knowledge is preserved so future investigators can begin where previous investigators ended. The location converts observation into institutional memory without pretending that uncertainty has been eliminated.

## GM Guidance
Never make the facility sinister for its own sake. Havel and the Program are not omniscient. The archive demonstrates prior awareness of patterns, not complete understanding.

The final debrief should focus on the quality of investigator reasoning, confidence, and recommendation rather than whether they selected a preferred outcome.

## Operational Notes
This location is deliberately separate from the runway observation, which remains an operational area within L-01. Preserving this distinction keeps existing canonical references stable.
