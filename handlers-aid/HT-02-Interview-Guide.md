# HT-02 — Interview Guide

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

## Purpose

HT-02 is the canonical interview subsystem for Operation 2. It provides shared Handler guidance and navigation to the ten individual interview guides. The subsystem derives its method from [HT-01 — Live Interview Guide](HT-01-Live-Interview-Guide.md).

The interviews are designed as investigative encounters, not transcripts and not clue dispensers. Each witness reveals a way of thinking, a human baseline, and a consequence that may alter the investigation.

## Shared Handler Guidance

### Preserve Cognitive Voice

Each witness has a distinct method of reasoning. Preserve that method even when investigators ask unexpected questions or pursue the conversation in a different order.

### Do Not Force the Script

Quoted exchanges demonstrate likely answers and rhythms. They are not a required sequence. Use the guide to answer the investigators’ actual questions.

### Separate Observation from Interpretation

Victims remember experiences but not reliable geography. Supporting witnesses establish before-and-after baselines. Neither group can identify the Mi-Go chamber through testimony alone.

### Let Interviews Create Consequences

The investigators’ methods affect trust, cooperation, later decisions, and institutional pressure. Review each guide’s Handler Notes and its related scene after play.

### Preserve Moral Ambiguity

The returned victims are healthier, calmer, or more socially comfortable. The improvement is real. So is the violation of agency. Do not resolve that contradiction for the players.

## Canonical Structure

Every individual guide uses the following structure:

- **Default Timing**, where timing materially affects presentation;
- **General Guidance**;
- **Interview**;
- **Handler Notes**.

Additional headings may appear when an interview contains a meaningful state change, such as David Barlow’s mid-interview shift or the surrender of his revolver.
