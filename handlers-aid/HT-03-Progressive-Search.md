# HT-03 — Locating the Mi-Go Chamber

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

I'd actually formalize this as a Handler Tool.
Not Mechanics.
Not Scene.
Something like:
HT-03 — Locating the Mi-Go Chamber
or
Handler Tool
Progressive Search Resolution
This becomes reusable for future operations.

I also think we should change the mental model.
Instead of:
Searching for the chamber
They're actually searching for:
The Pattern
The chamber is simply where the pattern ends.
Everything they've learned allows them to progressively eliminate more and more of the forest.

Progressive Search Model
Instead of binary success/failure, every investigation contributes Search Advantages.
Not points.
Advantages.
Advantages permit additional approaches.

Example:
Initially they have:
terrain
trails
maps
That's enough to wander around.
They'll never find it.

Later they add:
Whitaker recording
↓
Now they know they're looking for an area where the interference changes.

Later:
Samantha Journal
↓
Now they know approximately where unusual terrain exists.

Later:
Nate GPS
↓
Now they know the likely approach vector.

Later:
Kowalski Radio
↓
Now they can actively track signal strength.
Notice what's happening.
They're no longer "rolling higher."
They're changing the problem.
I love that.

Then I'd define Search States.
Rather than:
+6
+7
+8
Think:

State 0
Random searching.
No chance.
Forest feels enormous.

State 1
The investigators know approximately where to search.
Still hundreds of acres.

State 2
The investigators isolate one ridgeline.
Search becomes feasible.

State 3
The investigators identify the correct drainage/overlook.
They're close.
Now environmental conditions matter.
Night.
Fog.
Storm.
Time pressure.

State 4
They locate the anomaly.
Not the chamber.
The anomaly.
This is important.
Imagine:
The radio suddenly changes.
Birdsong stops.
Trees feel...
wrong.
They've found the place.
Not the entrance.

State 5
They recognize the chamber.
This is huge.
Imagine finding the clearing...
...and walking past it.
Because it doesn't look remarkable.
It looks...
ordered.
If they don't understand what they're looking for...
they leave.

That feels incredibly Delta Green.

State 6
The investigators discover the chamber while occupied.
Default outcome.

State 7
The investigators arrive moments after the Mi-Go leaves.
Different clues.
Victims.
Equipment.
No confrontation.

State 8
The investigators detect the Mi-Go before it detects them.
Rare.
Allows observation.

State 9
The investigators surprise the Mi-Go.
Very unlikely.
Only possible through exceptional investigation.

State 10
Perfect operational approach.
Honestly...
I don't think this should exist.
I think the Mi-Go should always retain initiative somewhere.
Maybe the best possible outcome is simply:
The investigators begin the encounter on their terms.
That's enough.

One thing I'd absolutely add
You touched on something brilliant without quite saying it.
Finding the chamber should not be the only successful search result.
Sometimes they should discover things like:
Fresh boot prints.
Recently broken vegetation.
A strange soil sample.
Unusual insect behavior.
A snapped radio antenna.
An area where every compass behaves oddly.
Search tape placed by another team.
An abandoned survey stake.
Evidence Samantha had recently passed through.
Evidence Kowalski had stopped.
These don't advance them immediately.
They generate entirely new investigations.
Maybe they take soil back to the FBI.
Maybe Hal studies the radio anomaly.
Maybe Brooks compares it to another case.
The forest becomes a producer of evidence instead of simply containing the ending.

I think this becomes one of Episode 2's defining Handler Tools.
I'd write it almost like a game engine:
Handler Tool

Progressive Search Resolution

Purpose

The Mi-Go Chamber is never located through a single successful Search roll.

Instead, investigators accumulate independent lines of evidence that progressively constrain the search area.

Each significant discovery unlocks new investigative approaches.

Each successful search changes the nature of subsequent searches.

The Handler should think in terms of reducing uncertainty rather than rewarding high die rolls.

By the time the chamber is found, the investigators should feel they have earned the discovery through synthesis, comparison, and careful observation—not luck.
