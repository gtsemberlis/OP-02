# HT-01 — Live Interview Guide

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

## Purpose

HT-01 establishes the Handler’s method for presenting interviews in Operation 2. The individual interview guides are collected under [HT-02 — Interview Guide](HT-02-Interview-Guide.md).

Interviews are not neutral information-gathering exercises. Every interview changes the investigation. Someone becomes suspicious. Someone becomes hopeful. Someone revisits old evidence. Someone contacts the police. Someone returns to the forest.

The Handler should treat every question as an action with consequences.

## Core Principles

### People Reveal Experiences

Returned victims reliably remember intentions, observations, emotions, and isolated sensory impressions. They do not reliably remember geography. Places are discovered through evidence. People reveal experiences.

### Observation Before Interpretation

Witnesses distinguish among:

- what they observed;
- what they inferred;
- what they cannot know.

The Handler should preserve those distinctions. Do not improve a witness’s certainty for the sake of clarity.

### Distinct Cognitive Voices

Each witness reasons differently. Their identity should emerge through how they organize uncertainty, not through accents, catchphrases, or theatrical dialogue alone.

### Investigative Function

No interview exists solely to distribute clues. Each interview teaches a different investigative lesson, establishes a human baseline, changes the social landscape, or reframes the operation’s moral problem.

### Imposed Improvement

The returned victims are objectively improved in subtle ways. Those improvements occurred without their knowledge or consent. Supporting witnesses may welcome the outcome, fear the process, or experience both reactions at once. The contradiction should remain unresolved.

## Using HT-02

Begin with [HT-02 — Interview Guide](HT-02-Interview-Guide.md), which provides navigation, shared Handler guidance, and links to every individual interview.

Each individual guide follows the canonical structure:

1. **Default Timing** where timing materially affects the interview.
2. **General Guidance** describing cognitive voice and presentation.
3. **Interview** providing likely exchanges without requiring a fixed script.
4. **Handler Notes** explaining investigative purpose, consequences, and thematic function.

The quoted exchanges are models, not mandatory dialogue. Investigators may ask questions in any order. Preserve the witness’s reasoning and factual limits even when the wording changes.

## Consequences

After an interview, consult the related scene and personnel records. The timeline advances because people make decisions, not because the calendar changes.

Examples include:

- Christopher begins reviewing old maps and equipment.
- Rebecca contacts Chief Patrick O’Connor if investigators become repetitive or accusatory.
- Nathaniel contacts SaucerWatch and shares his analysis.
- Samantha decides to revisit the forest.
- David’s fear may escalate into the purchase of a revolver, or trust may lead him to surrender it.

These are not punishments. They are consequences created by attention.
