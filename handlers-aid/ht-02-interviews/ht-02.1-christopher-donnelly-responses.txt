HT-02.1 — Interview Guide: Christopher Donnelly
General Guidance

Chris is patient, thoughtful, and deliberate.

He rarely answers immediately. Long pauses are normal as he carefully separates what he remembers from what he assumes. If he is uncertain, he says so plainly.

Investigators who attempt to rush him often receive shorter answers. Those who allow him time to think frequently receive additional details he recalls while speaking.

He often gestures with his hands or sketches simple diagrams rather than relying on abstract descriptions.

"Can you walk me through the day you disappeared?"

"It was a Saturday."

"I wasn't working."

"I told Jen I was heading out for a few hours."

"Loaded the truck. Radio gear. Coffee."

"I had a couple places in mind."

"I don't honestly know which one I ended up choosing."
A pause.

"I wanted to get away from the house for a bit."

"Where were you planning to hike?"

Chris:

"Somewhere with enough room to put an antenna up."

"I usually keep a few spots in mind."

"Couldn't tell you which one I actually drove to."

After a pause:

"That bothers me."

"Was anything unusual before you disappeared?"

He thinks.

"No."

Another pause.

"Nothing I'd have noticed at the time."

"What's the last thing you clearly remember?"

"Walking."

"Trees."

"Checking the radio."

"Stopping once or twice."

"Couldn't tell you where."

"Do you remember leaving the trail?"

"No."

"If I had decided to, I'd probably remember making the decision."

He frowns slightly.

"I don't."

"Did you hear anything unusual?"

"The radio wasn't right."

"Interference."

"Not constant."

"Changing."

He makes a small motion with one hand.

"Like it was fading in and out without any reason."

"Did you see anyone?"

"No."

He waits a moment.

"I don't remember seeing anybody."

"That's different."

"Different how?"

"If I say I didn't see anyone..."

"That means I remember looking."

"I don't."

"I just don't remember."

"Did you feel lost?"

"No."

He answers almost immediately.

"Confused later."

"Not lost."

"What do you remember next?"

"I woke up sitting down."

"Back against something."

"Ground was damp."

"Didn't really pay attention to where I was."

Pause.

"I probably should have."

"Were you injured?"

"No."

"Hungry."

"Stiff."

"Like I'd been sitting still for a long time."

"Did you dream?"

He considers the question.

Longer than any previous one.

"No."

A pause.

"At least..."

"Not that I remember."

"Have you noticed any changes since you came home?"

He smiles awkwardly.

"Apparently."

"Like what?"

"I quit smoking."

He says it almost apologetically.

"Just stopped."

"Didn't decide to."

"Didn't try."

"Just..."

He shrugs.

"Didn't want another cigarette."

"Does that bother you?"

He looks down at the table.

"The quitting?"

"No."

Another pause.

"The not understanding why..."

"Yeah."

"Anything else?"

"Jen says I'm calmer."

"Kids say I'm around more."

A small smile.

"They're probably right."

"Do you feel different?"

This produces the longest silence of the interview.

"Not really."

"I still feel like me."

He looks genuinely troubled.

"Which makes it hard to explain why everybody else thinks something changed."

"Why do you think that happened?"

He immediately shakes his head.

"I don't."

Another pause.

"I've got ideas."

"You asked what I think."

"Not what I know."

"Then what do you know?"

He leans back.

"Time passed."

"I don't remember it."

"Something happened."

"I came back."

"Everything after that..."

He spreads his hands.

"That's where the guessing starts."

"Is there anything you've remembered that you haven't told anyone?"

He thinks for nearly half a minute.

"Not remembered."

"Realized."

The investigators wait.

"I keep trying to fit what happened into something I already understand."

"Missing time."

"Concussion."

"Getting lost."

"Medical problem."

He slowly shakes his head.

"None of them fit."

"What worries you the most?"

He doesn't answer immediately.

"I machine parts."

"If one dimension is wrong..."

"Everything built on it is wrong."

Another pause.

"I don't know which part of my memory is the bad dimension."

"So I don't know what else might not fit."

Handler Notes

Chris is the operation's most disciplined witness. His value is not that he remembers more than the other victims, but that he consistently distinguishes observation from interpretation. When investigators ask speculative questions, he often reframes his answer by separating what he knows from what he merely suspects. This habit makes his testimony unusually trustworthy and rewards careful interviewing.

One subtle change in his post-return demeanor should become apparent over the course of the interview. He is still quiet and methodical, but there is a warmth that wasn't there before. He smiles more readily when speaking about his children, mentions Jennifer naturally rather than as an afterthought, and seems less inclined to retreat emotionally. Like his abrupt cessation of smoking, these changes are objectively positive—yet they leave both Chris and his family with the unsettling realization that something fundamental has been altered without his knowledge or consent.
