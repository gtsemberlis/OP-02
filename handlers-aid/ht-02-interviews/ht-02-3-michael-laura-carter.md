# HT-02.3 — Interview Guide — Michael & Laura Carter

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

## Default Timing

This guide assumes investigators interview Michael and Laura during Act I (June 27–July 1), shortly after Samantha's return. Samantha is home, physically healthy, and adjusting to ordinary life. Her parents know nothing about the Unnatural and have only limited knowledge of the other disappearances.

## General Guidance

Michael and Laura should almost always be interviewed together.

Michael naturally answers questions about events, timelines, and observations.

Laura answers questions about Samantha herself.

Neither interrupts the other. Instead, they unconsciously complete each other's thoughts after decades of marriage.

When they disagree, they do so gently—and those disagreements often reveal more than either answer alone.

## Interview
"Can you tell us about Samantha?"

Michael answers first.

"She's always wanted to know how things work."

"When she was eight she took apart a clock."

Laura smiles.

"She put it back together, too."

Michael nods.

"Mostly."

They both laugh.

"What was she like before she disappeared?"

Michael thinks.

"Independent."

"Exceptionally bright."

"Probably smarter than I ever was."

Laura smiles warmly.

"And funny."

Michael immediately nods.

"Yes."

A pause.

"People forget that."

"What kinds of things was she interested in?"

"Engineering."

Michael answers.

"Electronics."

"Radio."

"Photography."

Laura adds:

"Books."

"Museums."

"Astronomy."

"Anything that let her understand how the world fit together."

"Did she spend much time away from home?"

"Enough."

Michael shrugs.

"College."

"Radio club."

"Hiking."

"Pretty normal."

Laura smiles.

"She always texted if she'd be late."

"Did anything seem unusual before she disappeared?"

Michael shakes his head.

"No."

Then pauses.

"Different, maybe."

"Different how?"

"She seemed..."

He searches for the word.

"More confident."

Before he can continue—

Laura quietly says,

"Not confident."

Michael looks at her.

"Comfortable."

She says.

"That's different."

A long silence.

"What do you mean?"

Laura thinks for several seconds.

"She used to apologize."

"Constantly."

"Interrupting."

"Talking too much."

"Talking too little."

A faint smile.

"Existing."

Another pause.

"Then..."

"She stopped."

"Stopped apologizing?"

Laura nods.

"She didn't become louder."

"She didn't become someone else."

"She just..."

She shrugs.

"Stopped assuming she was bothering everyone."

"Would you call that confidence?"

Laura gently shakes her head.

"No."

"Confidence is something you build."

"This felt..."

She struggles.

"Like something heavy wasn't there anymore."

Michael quietly listens.

He doesn't argue.

"Did Samantha ever tell you why?"

They both answer at the same time.

"No."

They exchange a small smile.

"Can you tell us about the day she disappeared?"

Michael answers.

"Ordinary."

"She packed a backpack."

"Said she'd be out for the day."

"That was it."

"Did she tell you where she was going?"

"Not specifically."

Michael shrugs.

"Somewhere to hike."

"Somewhere to use the radio."

Laura adds,

"She had several places she liked."

"I honestly couldn't tell you which one she planned to visit."

"When did you realize something was wrong?"

Laura answers immediately.

"That evening."

"She always answered texts."

"Always."

A pause.

"She didn't."

"Did you think she'd run away?"

Michael looks genuinely confused.

"No."

Laura quietly echoes him.

"Never."

"Why not?"

Michael answers.

"Because she liked her life."

Laura finishes.

"And she knew we'd help if something was wrong."

"How has she been since she came home?"

Michael answers first.

"Better."

Then he hesitates.

"That's what worries me."

"Better how?"

"More eye contact."

He immediately looks embarrassed.

"That sounds strange."

"She always looked at people."

"Now..."

He gestures vaguely.

"She's actually inside the conversation."

Laura watches him for a moment.

"She laughs sooner."

"She doesn't second-guess every sentence."

"She's..."

A pause.

"Gentler with herself."

"Does that make you happy?"

Laura answers softly.

"Of course."

Another pause.

"And it breaks my heart."

"Why?"

"Because she didn't earn that change."

The room is quiet.

"It was done to her."

"Has Samantha said anything about what happened?"

Michael shakes his head.

"She says she doesn't remember."

Laura nods.

"I believe her."

"Do you think she's hiding anything?"

Both answer immediately.

"No."

"What worries you the most?"

Michael thinks carefully.

"That there's an explanation."

"And we just haven't found it yet."

Laura looks at Samantha's closed bedroom door.

"I'm afraid there isn't."

Neither argues.

They've clearly had this conversation before.

"Is there anything else we should know?"

Michael looks toward Laura.

She nods for him to answer first.

"Please don't treat her like she's broken."

Laura quietly adds,

"She's trying very hard to convince herself she's the same person."

A long silence.

"I think she wants that to be true more than anyone."

## Handler Notes

Michael and Laura should feel like two halves of a complete picture. Michael instinctively catalogs observable changes—eye contact, behavior, routines—while Laura notices shifts in Samantha's inner life and emotional habits. Neither perspective is sufficient on its own, but together they describe the same transformation with remarkable clarity.

The interview's emotional centerpiece is the distinction between confidence and comfort. Michael initially interprets Samantha's post-return behavior as growing self-assurance. Laura recognizes something subtler: Samantha did not overcome her insecurities through experience or maturity. It is as though the insecurities themselves have been quietly removed. Neither parent can explain why this realization feels unsettling, but together they articulate one of the operation's central horrors: not that Samantha has changed, but that some of her emotional burdens have simply ceased to exist without her participation or consent.
