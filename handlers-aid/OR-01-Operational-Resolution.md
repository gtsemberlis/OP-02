# Operational Resolution
## The Mi-Go Presence

If investigators locate the chamber, the Mi-Go installation ceases to function by the conclusion of the operation. Whether destroyed during the confrontation, dismantled by surviving Mi-Go, or rendered inaccessible after their departure, the chamber no longer serves as an active operational site.

If a Mi-Go body is recovered, Delta Green dispatches a Retrieval Team to assume custody. Investigators are neither expected nor authorized to conduct long-term examination of recovered biological material.

Recovered technology—including brain cylinders or other devices—is similarly transferred to Program custody whenever practical. Investigators should not be expected to understand their purpose or operation, only to secure it until relieved by the Program.

## Official Resolution

The disappearances are ultimately resolved through conventional investigative means.

Working under their FBI authority, the investigators submit reports sufficient to satisfy state and local authorities and formally close the investigation. The exact explanation is left to the Handler but should be credible enough to prevent further institutional scrutiny while avoiding unnecessary public attention.

Media interest fades quickly following the Independence Day search effort. Within weeks, the story has largely disappeared from public attention.

No official report acknowledges the existence of the Unnatural.

## The Community

The Mohawk Valley Amateur Radio Club survives.

Meetings resume.

Attendance gradually returns to normal.

Members remain grateful simply to have one another back.

The subtle personality changes exhibited by the returned victims do not noticeably regress. Over time, those changes simply become accepted as part of who they are now—a quiet reminder that whatever happened in the forest did not end when they came home.

Rick Holloway continues marking attendance before every meeting.

Eventually, he stops noticing the empty chairs.

## Samantha Carter

If Samantha survives the operation, she resumes her studies at Mohawk Valley Community College and gradually returns to club activities. Friends, family, and classmates quietly accept that she has changed, even if they cannot explain how.

If Samantha is not recovered, her disappearance casts a long shadow over both the college and the amateur radio club. Her absence becomes the first empty chair that no one expects to fill.

## SaucerWatch

Following the July 4 search effort, SaucerWatch gradually shifts its attention elsewhere.

If investigators maintained a constructive relationship with Evan Brooks, he may remain a useful unofficial source for rumors, civilian reports, and fringe claims in future operations. He never learns the truth, but he gains a healthy respect for investigators who treated him honestly.

If relations deteriorated, SaucerWatch continues to speculate publicly, though without sufficient evidence to meaningfully interfere with Delta Green's containment efforts.

## Delta Green Assessment

Operation Carrier Wave is considered operationally successful if the active Unnatural threat is neutralized or no longer presents an ongoing danger to civilians.

The Program's long-term interest focuses on any recovered biological material, technological artifacts, radio recordings, or anomalous evidence secured during the investigation. Those materials quietly enter the Program's archive for future study and comparison.

No new Friendly is expected to emerge from this operation. Most witnesses lack both the knowledge and the disposition to become reliable Program assets.

## Aftermath

Although the immediate crisis has passed, the operation leaves lasting consequences.

Recovered Mi-Go technology may remain under Program study for years. Radio recordings, engineering observations, and other technical evidence become part of Delta Green's growing archive of comparative cases.

If investigators earned Evan Brooks' trust, he may remain a valuable civilian contact capable of recognizing unusual patterns or directing attention toward future incidents without ever understanding their true nature.

Life gradually returns to routine throughout the Mohawk Valley. The investigation ends. The search teams leave. SaucerWatch moves on. The amateur radio club continues meeting each month.

The returned victims continue living outwardly ordinary lives. Their families slowly stop noticing the small changes that once seemed so remarkable, accepting them simply as part of who they are now.

Only the investigators know that whatever happened in the forest did not truly end when they came home.
