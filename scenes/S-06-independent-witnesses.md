# S-06 — Independent Witnesses

## Chapter

Chapter 3 — Corroboration

## Scene Summary

Investigators interview survivors and observers whose incomplete accounts independently support the technical evidence.

## Scene Purpose

Reward attention, broaden the human perspective, and provide non-technical corroboration without a single decisive witness.

## Primary Location(s)

L-04 — NTSB Field Office

## Participants

N-06 Karen Delgado; N-08 Emily Chen; N-09 Maria Alvarez; N-10 Daniel Price; N-11 Sarah Walker; N-12 Owen Walker; N-14 Lucas Bennett

## Estimated Runtime

45–75 minutes

## Investigation Phase

Independent Corroboration

## Scene Flow

1. Delgado organizes access and procedural context.
2. Witnesses describe timing, motion, crew reaction, and unusual avoidance behavior from different perspectives.
3. No adult supplies a complete supernatural account.
4. Owen's drawing depicts the broad manta-like runway shape if investigators notice or ask to see it.

## Key Information

- The crew reacted to instrument agreement, not a fleeting visual impression.
- Several witnesses noticed motion or turbulence inconsistent with an empty runway.
- Details align across people who did not coordinate their accounts.
- Owen's drawing resembles the large form inferred from Rao's data.

## Evidence Available

H-04 — NTSB Witness Briefing Sheets; A-06 — Owen Walker's Drawing; witness statements

## Likely Investigator Approaches

HUMINT, careful listening, timeline comparison, child-sensitive interviewing, and attention to incidental material.

## GM Guidance

Do not gate the drawing behind a perfect question. Reward investigators who notice Owen, his papers, or Sarah's concern.

## Before Leaving This Scene

Investigators should have human corroboration independent of Alexandria.

## Quick Reference

No witness solves the case; together they eliminate coincidence.
