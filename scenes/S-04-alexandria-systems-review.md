# S-04 — Alexandria Systems Review

## Chapter

Chapter 2 — Independent Analysis

## Scene Summary

Investigators examine raw incidents, internal tests, and physical operating conditions to determine whether Alexandria's anomaly is reproducible.

## Scene Purpose

Convert suspicion into verified evidence and introduce the phenomenon's wider ecology.

## Primary Location(s)

L-02 — Alexandria Aviation Systems Headquarters

## Participants

N-03 Dr. Ananya Rao; N-04 Kevin Park; N-05 Javier Ortiz

## Estimated Runtime

45–75 minutes

## Investigation Phase

Technical Verification

## Scene Flow

1. Compare unrelated obstruction events.
2. Review modality timestamps and confidence thresholds.
3. Inspect equipment, access logs, and data provenance.
4. Observe the bird-avoidance pattern and discuss controlled testing.

## Key Information

- The incidents are not copies of one another and do not share a simple data-source failure.
- Confidence increases when more modalities are added rather than collapsing.
- The anomaly appears in multiple forms and scales.
- Active Alexandria systems correlate with subtle animal avoidance.

## Evidence Available

A-04 — Runway Obstruction Event Summary

## Likely Investigator Approaches

Computer Science, SIGINT, Engineering, Search, Bureaucracy, and physical inspection.

## GM Guidance

Allow investigators to disprove mundane explanations. Failure should cost time or create uncertainty, not hide the core truth.

## Before Leaving This Scene

The investigators should accept that the system is observing a real external phenomenon.

## Quick Reference

Conclusion: real signal, uncertain nature, no demonstrated hostile intent.
