# SC-04 — Christopher Donnelly Residence

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

## Purpose
Introduce Christopher Donnelly and Jennifer Donnelly, establish Christopher's systems-oriented approach to the disappearances, and provide investigators with one of the operation's richest sources of engineering analysis.
The investigators' interaction with Christopher directly influences his own parallel investigation.

## Scene Overview
Christopher Donnelly's home is quiet.
Not empty.
Purposeful.
Everything inside reflects someone who solves problems by understanding how systems work.
The television is off.
The workshop is active.
Whiteboards hold diagrams instead of family calendars.
Maps cover workbenches beside electronic test equipment.
Unlike other victims' homes, Christopher's residence does not feel frozen by tragedy.
It feels occupied by someone trying to understand what happened.
His investigation has already begun.
The investigators simply become part of it.

## What Can The Investigators Learn?
This scene explains how Christopher investigates.
Christopher's Perspective
Christopher approaches the disappearances as an engineering problem.
Rather than asking who is responsible, he asks:
"What physical system could produce these effects?"
His analysis compares:
utility infrastructure;
communications networks;
weather records;
terrain;
maintenance activity;
signal propagation.
He is not chasing conspiracies.
He is attempting to eliminate every ordinary explanation before considering extraordinary ones.

Jennifer's Perspective
Jennifer confirms the ordinary life investigators expect to find.
Healthy marriage.
Stable employment.
Good relationship with their children.
No financial pressure.
No hidden conflicts.
She also provides the investigators' first clear account that Christopher returned subtly changed.
At first, those changes seemed like blessings.
Only later did they become unsettling.

Christopher After His Return
Jennifer and Christopher independently describe several subtle but unmistakable changes following his return.
The most dramatic is also the most ordinary.
Christopher had smoked approximately a pack of cigarettes a day for nearly twenty years.
One morning after returning home, he simply stopped.
No nicotine patches.
No withdrawal symptoms.
No cravings.
When Jennifer asked why, he shrugged.
"I just don't feel like it anymore."
Initially, the family celebrated the change.
Only later did it begin to feel unsettling.
Jennifer also noticed that Christopher:
slept more soundly;
seemed calmer under stress;
spent more time with their children;
smiled more easily.
None of these changes appeared harmful.
Quite the opposite.
That is precisely what troubles her.
She eventually tells the investigators:
"I kept waiting for him to start smoking again."
A long pause.
"He never even wanted one."
This is the investigators' first clear indication that the disappearances may leave victims subtly—but profoundly—altered in ways conventional medicine cannot explain.

The Workshop
Christopher's workshop demonstrates disciplined thinking.
Every rejected hypothesis remains documented.
Every conclusion is supported by observations.
Investigators quickly recognize someone who distinguishes carefully between evidence and speculation.

## What Can Happen In This Scene?
These are interactive outcomes rather than pieces of information.

Interview Christopher
Christopher willingly discusses his observations and theories.
He is honest about uncertainty.
When he says,
"I don't know."
the investigators can trust that he means it.

Interview Jennifer
Jennifer provides the emotional context Christopher rarely expresses.
Her observations reinforce that the disappearances interrupted an ordinary, healthy family rather than revealing hidden dysfunction.

Examine The Workshop
Investigators may spend considerable time studying Christopher's notes, maps, equipment, and engineering models.
Many ideas prove incorrect.
Several independently reinforce conclusions investigators have reached elsewhere.

Compare Evidence
Christopher eagerly compares his own observations against information provided by investigators.
This is one of the few locations where sharing information may substantially improve a civilian's understanding of events.
It also encourages Christopher to continue investigating independently.

Encourage—or Discourage—His Investigation
The investigators' questions influence Christopher's future actions.
If encouraged, he expands his research into communications infrastructure, utility records, and terrain surrounding Hogsback.
If strongly discouraged, he remains cautious—but never entirely abandons the investigation.
Either outcome reflects Christopher's own decisions rather than arbitrary timeline advancement.

## Scene Progression
### Initial Visit
Christopher treats the investigation as an engineering problem.
He believes the correct explanation exists.
He simply hasn't found it yet.

### Mid Investigation
The workshop becomes noticeably busier.
Whiteboards fill.
Maps accumulate annotations.
New engineering models appear.
Christopher increasingly searches for patterns rather than isolated failures.

### Late Investigation
Christopher reluctantly acknowledges that conventional engineering no longer explains every observation.
His thinking remains disciplined.
He has not embraced extraordinary conclusions.
He has merely exhausted ordinary ones.
That realization clearly troubles him.

## Handler Notes
Christopher Donnelly's residence represents systems thinking.
Where Samantha Carter records observations, Hal Whitaker analyzes signals, and Nathaniel Collins models technical systems, Christopher attempts to understand the disappearances through the interaction of physical infrastructure and the environment. His workshop is not filled with conspiracy theories; it is filled with carefully documented failures of ordinary explanations.
This scene also reinforces one of the campaign's locked principles:
Investigators create consequences simply by asking questions.
Christopher is already curious when the investigators arrive. Speaking with them does not reveal hidden information—it validates his belief that the disappearances deserve serious analysis. As a result, he quietly begins conducting his own parallel investigation. He gathers records, revisits assumptions, and moves ever closer to the same geographic patterns the investigators are uncovering.
Christopher's sudden cessation of a decades-long smoking habit should initially appear to be an ordinary human success story. Only after investigators begin hearing similar accounts from the other victims do they recognize it as part of a larger pattern. The horror is not that Christopher became healthier—it is that the change occurred without struggle, without conscious choice, and without his informed consent.
This quietly introduces one of Operation Carrier Wave's central questions:
If an external force removes suffering by rewriting part of a person's identity, has it healed them—or violated them?
Christopher behaves exactly as a competent engineer would when confronted with evidence that refuses to fit existing models. He never becomes obsessed, paranoid, or reckless. He simply continues gathering data. That professionalism makes him one of the operation's most valuable civilian allies—and one of its most vulnerable.
