# S-02 — Program Briefing

## Chapter

Chapter 1 — Assignment

## Scene Summary

The Program assigns the investigators to determine whether the obstruction reports are genuine, dangerous, containable, and compatible with Alexandria's continued operation.

## Scene Purpose

Define authority, scope, constraints, and the Program's reactive posture.

## Primary Location(s)

L-05 — Program Secure Briefing Facility

## Participants

N-01 Michael Havel; investigators

## Estimated Runtime

15–25 minutes

## Investigation Phase

Assignment

## Scene Flow

1. Review the O'Hare event and related anomaly reports.
2. State the operational objectives and cover identities.
3. Clarify that the Program has no settled explanation.
4. Provide access to Alexandria and relevant federal partners.

## Key Information

- O'Hare is one of several geographically separated incidents.
- Three reports involve military airfields; others occurred over the Atlantic and in Alaska.
- The common factor is independent multi-modal confirmation.
- The Program wants assessment and containment, not automatic destruction.

## Evidence Available

A-01 — Initial Program Briefing

## Likely Investigator Approaches

Questions about authority, known precedent, technical support, legal cover, and acceptable outcomes.

## GM Guidance

Do not portray the Program as omniscient. Havel knows the pattern qualifies for attention, not what it means.

## Before Leaving This Scene

Investigators should have a clear mandate and a reason to begin with Alexandria.

## Quick Reference

Objectives: verify, characterize, contain, evaluate continuation.
