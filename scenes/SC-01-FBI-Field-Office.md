# SC-01 — FBI Field Office

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

## Purpose
 Introduce investigators to the official investigation and establish the baseline facts of the operation.

## Scene Overview
A temporary FBI command center has been established inside a small field office outside Utica. Folding tables, evidence boards, county maps, search schedules, and stacks of case binders fill nearly every available workspace. Agents move with the quiet efficiency of an investigation that has already consumed weeks of effort.
Nothing about the room suggests panic.
Instead, it communicates something more unsettling:
The Bureau has done everything right.
They simply have no answers.
Special Agent Elena Alvarez has been expecting the investigators' arrival. She has already assembled copies of the complete preliminary case file and intends to bring them up to speed before they begin conducting their own interviews.

## What Can The Investigators Learn?
This section is aggregated knowledge, not new writing.
By speaking with Elena, reviewing the report, and examining the office, investigators can learn:
The Investigation
The FBI has already conducted an extensive investigation.
Five disappearances have occurred.
Four victims have returned.
Nathaniel Collins' impossible travel data triggered Program attention.
No conventional explanation currently fits the evidence.

The Victims
The investigators receive:
victim histories
family information
interview summaries
employment records
educational records
timelines
The Amateur Radio Club immediately stands out as the only obvious connection shared by every victim.

Search Operations
The investigators learn:
where searches have occurred
how extensive those searches were
resources committed
why investigators believe the victims were not simply lost in the woods

Official Theory
There isn't one.
Every conventional explanation has significant shortcomings.
This is precisely why the investigators have been assigned.

## What Can Happen In This Scene?
This is where scenes become unique.
These are interactive outcomes, not information.

Elena Answers Questions
Investigators may ask Elena nearly any procedural question.
If the FBI already investigated something, Elena usually knows the answer or can direct investigators toward the appropriate documentation.

Investigators Develop Initial Leads
Players will naturally begin prioritizing:
which victim to interview first
whether to visit families
whether to investigate the radio club
whether to examine the search area
This scene establishes the investigation's initial direction.

The Investigation Begins
The players receive authority.
Vehicles.
Case files.
Agency cooperation.
From this point forward the investigation becomes player-directed.

Elena's Assessment
Throughout the conversation, Elena quietly forms opinions about the investigators.
Professional investigators who review evidence before acting earn her confidence.
Agents who immediately jump to unsupported conclusions may find Elena less forthcoming later in the operation.
Nothing about this is punitive.
It simply reflects two professionals learning to trust one another.

Returning To The Field Office
The investigators are expected to revisit this location throughout the operation.
Possible reasons include:
reviewing new evidence
requesting forensic support
updating Alvarez
comparing discoveries against FBI records
coordinating the expanding search
The scene evolves as the investigation progresses.

## Scene Progression
### Initial Visit
Primarily briefing and orientation.

### Mid Investigation
Functions as an investigative headquarters.
New evidence accumulates.
Alvarez may have updated reports.

### Late Investigation
The office shifts toward operational coordination.
Resources are redirected toward Daniel Kowalski's disappearance.
Search activity intensifies.
If the investigation reaches July 4, the atmosphere becomes increasingly strained as pressure mounts from local authorities, media attention, and the growing scale of the search.

## Handler Notes
This scene establishes one of the operation's core philosophical ideas:
The FBI has not failed. They have reached the limits of conventional investigation.
The investigators should leave impressed by the Bureau's professionalism rather than frustrated by incompetence. Every obvious lead has already been pursued. Every reasonable search has already been conducted.
That realization reframes the entire operation.
The investigators are not here to do a better investigation.
They are here to ask different questions.
