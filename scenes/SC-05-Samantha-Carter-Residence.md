# SC-05 — Samantha Carter Residence

**LOCKED CANONICAL**

## Purpose
 Provide investigators with their first direct opportunity to interview Samantha Carter, understand the human consequences of the disappearances, and obtain one of the operation's most valuable investigative artifacts.

## Scene Overview
The Carter residence is an ordinary middle-class home in a quiet neighborhood outside Utica. It feels comfortable, lived-in, and deeply familiar—a place where routine has been carefully reconstructed after a profound disruption.
Fresh coffee sits on the kitchen counter. Family photographs line the hallway. The television quietly reports local news in the background. Nothing appears outwardly unusual.
And yet everyone inside behaves as though they are carefully performing normal life rather than actually living it.
Michael Carter answers questions through facts and analysis.
Laura Carter answers them through emotion and memory.
Samantha sits somewhere between them—healthy, cooperative, thoughtful, and quietly frustrated that she cannot remember what happened to her.
Unlike many scenes in the operation, investigators are not confronting suspicion here.
They are being welcomed into someone's home.
That hospitality makes the questions harder to ask.

## Artifacts Available
Samantha Carter's Field Journal
The single most important artifact available in this scene.
The journal documents months of careful observations concerning amateur radio activity, hiking trips, engineering experiments, and environmental anomalies. It rewards comparison with several other artifacts throughout the operation.
Whether Samantha willingly shares it depends almost entirely upon the investigators' ability to establish trust.

## What Can The Investigators Learn?
This section represents information collectively available through interviews, observation, and examination of the residence.
Samantha Herself
Investigators discover Samantha is:
intelligent,
observant,
technically gifted,
cooperative,
remarkably free of exaggeration.
She is genuinely trying to remember what happened.
She simply cannot.

Before the Disappearance
Investigators learn:
Samantha became increasingly involved with the Amateur Radio Club.
Hal Whitaker and Nate Collins served as genuine mentors.
Her interest in radio, engineering, and hiking developed naturally over several years.
Nothing suggests cult involvement, mental illness, or exploitation.

After the Disappearance
The investigators notice subtle but consistent changes.
Michael observes increased confidence.
Laura observes increased comfort.
Samantha herself struggles to explain the difference, eventually describing social interaction as simply feeling "quieter" than it once did.
Medical examinations reveal nothing abnormal.

The Field Journal
If investigators gain access to Samantha's notebook, they begin assembling one of the operation's strongest comparative datasets.
The notebook contains:
hiking observations,
environmental measurements,
radio anomalies,
photographs,
engineering notes,
incomplete questions,
carefully documented uncertainties.
Individually, none solve the mystery.
Collectively, they become increasingly valuable as additional evidence is recovered.

Hogsback State Forest
Samantha admits she cannot stop thinking about where she disappeared.
She believes there is something important she failed to understand.
She does not yet know what.
If investigators visit after July 2—or discover the latest entries in her journal—they realize her curiosity has become resolve. Samantha has decided to return to "where it happened" and has begun assembling equipment for an extended wilderness excursion. The list is practical, methodical, and unlike any of her previous hiking preparations, suggesting she expects uncertainty rather than recreation.

## What Can Happen In This Scene?
These are interactive outcomes rather than pieces of information.

Samantha Shares Her Journal
If investigators establish trust, Samantha willingly allows them to examine her field journal.
She does not believe it contains anything particularly important.
She is surprised by the investigators' interest.

Samantha Becomes An Ally
Investigators who treat Samantha respectfully gain more than information.
They gain another careful observer.
After the interview, Samantha continues examining her own experience and recording new observations.
Future visits may reveal additional entries.

Samantha Withholds The Journal
If investigators behave dismissively, invade her privacy, or treat her as an unreliable witness, Samantha becomes reluctant to cooperate.
She remains polite.
She simply decides her own investigation is more productive than theirs.

The Investigators Recognize The Risk
As conversations progress, investigators may begin realizing Samantha is not seeking closure.
She is trying to understand.
That distinction becomes increasingly important as the operation progresses.
Whether they choose to discourage her, recruit her assistance, or quietly monitor her may significantly influence later events.

## Scene Progression
### Initial Visit
The residence feels hopeful.
The family believes federal investigators may finally provide answers.
Samantha is curious and cooperative.
The field journal documents observations rather than intentions.

### Mid Investigation
The atmosphere becomes noticeably more uncertain.
Samantha has begun comparing her own observations against questions raised by the investigators.
New pages appear in her journal.
She speaks increasingly often about returning to Hogsback.
Her parents remain unaware of how determined she has become.

### Late Investigation
If Samantha has not yet left, investigators arrive to find preparations nearly complete.
Her room contains:
trail maps,
survival equipment,
handwritten checklists,
new journal entries,
carefully organized supplies.
She is no longer simply remembering.
She is planning.
If investigators arrive after her departure, the room feels abruptly incomplete.
Her backpack, boots, and field journal are gone.
A brief handwritten note apologizes to her parents and promises she will return soon.
The investigators immediately understand where she has gone.

## Handler Notes
This scene establishes what is personally at stake in Operation Airspace.
Every previous scene focuses on evidence.
This one focuses on the person behind the evidence.
The investigators should leave convinced that Samantha is neither unstable nor reckless. She is intelligent, methodical, and acting in good faith. That is precisely what makes her growing determination to return to Hogsback so unsettling.
Perhaps most importantly, this scene quietly transforms Samantha from a victim into an investigator. She is asking many of the same questions as the players, recording observations with the same discipline, and independently moving toward the same conclusions. If the investigators fail to recognize that parallel investigation—or fail to intervene when they see where it is leading—then her eventual disappearance is not simply the timeline advancing. It is the consequence of a thoughtful young woman making a reasoned decision based on the evidence available to her.
