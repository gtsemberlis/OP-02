# S-03 — Institutional Coordination

## Chapter

Chapter 2 — Alexandria

## Scene Summary

Investigators enter Alexandria under official aviation-safety cover, meet its leadership, and establish access to the system and its personnel.

## Scene Purpose

Present Alexandria as competent and non-malicious while exposing the breadth of the anomaly.

## Primary Location(s)

L-02 — Alexandria Aviation Systems Headquarters

## Participants

N-02 Ethan Mercer; N-04 Kevin Park; N-05 Javier Ortiz

## Estimated Runtime

35–60 minutes

## Investigation Phase

Access and Orientation

## Scene Flow

1. Mercer frames Alexandria's mission and reputational risk.
2. Park explains the sensor-fusion architecture and validation safeguards.
3. Ortiz establishes access limits and internal-security concerns.
4. Investigators review the technical overview and identify Rao as the person closest to the anomaly.

## Key Information

- Alexandria's system fuses radar, lidar, optical, infrared, and operational data.
- The alerts survived internal validation and were not generated by one defective modality.
- The company has attempted to reproduce, suppress, and explain the results.
- Bird activity around active equipment has changed in subtle but repeatable ways.

## Evidence Available

A-03 — Alexandria Technical Overview

## Likely Investigator Approaches

Technical questioning, corporate-record review, security inquiry, personnel interviews, and observation of active systems.

## GM Guidance

Alexandria should reward professional treatment. Suspicion is reasonable, but evidence should steadily undermine the idea of deliberate fraud.

## Before Leaving This Scene

Investigators should understand the system well enough to recognize why ordinary malfunction is inadequate.

## Quick Reference

Key contacts: Mercer, Park, Ortiz. Lead: Dr. Rao.
